Golf in McCall, Idaho ⛳

Mountain golf at 5,000 feet — Star's closest summer escape, two hours north into the pines

Why This Guide Exists

McCall sits in Idaho's high country at the southern edge of the Salmon River Mountains, 110 miles north of Star (about a 2-hour drive on Highway 55). It's a mountain resort town built around Payette Lake, originally a logging community, now a year-round playground for skiers, boaters, and golfers. For Treasure Valley golfers, it's the closest place to play real mountain golf without having to drive to Sun Valley or Coeur d'Alene.

The summer season is short — roughly mid-May through mid-October — but during those months the conditions are spectacular. Cool mornings (40–55°F), warm afternoons (70–80°F), almost no wind compared to the Treasure Valley, and pine-forest scenery with mountain backdrops on nearly every hole. The ball travels 8–10% farther at this elevation. Golf here feels different.

The downside: limited courses. McCall has three serious options and a couple of also-rans. But the three are good, the drive is beautiful, and a McCall weekend is one of the best summer escapes from Star.

McCall Golf: What You Need to Know

The Season

Best: July–August. Reliable warm weather, all courses fully open, gorgeous mountain conditions.

Spring (May–June): Variable. Lower-elevation courses (Jug Mountain) open earlier than higher ones (McCall GC). Mornings can be 40°F. Afternoons mid-60s.

Fall (September–early October): Stunning fall colors, fewer crowds, cool temperatures. The locals' favorite season.

Winter: Closed. McCall transitions to skiing (Brundage Mountain). Don't even ask about winter tee times.

The Landscape

Pine-forested mountains at 5,000 feet elevation. Payette Lake sits at the center of town, with most courses tucked into the valleys around it. Trees, water hazards, elevation changes, and views — but not the dramatic alpine drama of Sun Valley. This is friendly mountain golf.

The Vibe

McCall is small (3,500 year-round residents, swelling to 20,000+ in summer). It's a tourist town that locals also love. Pace of play is relaxed. Tee sheets get busy in July and August weekends — book ahead. Otherwise, walk-up play is usually possible.

Where to Stay

Downtown McCall has lakeside hotels (Shore Lodge is the iconic one), VRBOs and Airbnbs throughout the valley, and cabin rentals near the lake. McCall is small enough that nothing is more than 10 minutes from anything else.

The Best Courses

1. Jug Mountain Ranch ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The marquee round in McCall. Dramatic mountain layout with serious teeth.

The Experience: Built in 2002, Jug Mountain Ranch is the most ambitious golf course in central Idaho. The course winds through 1,400 acres of mountain meadows, pine forest, and creek valleys. Slope 138 from the tips is genuinely challenging — elevation changes, forced carries, water features, and tight tree-lined fairways punish poor shots. But the wide fairways from the forward tees give you a chance. Conditioning is excellent. The setting feels remote even though town is 10 minutes away. The 7th hole (signature par 3 over a meadow) and the 17th (downhill par 5 toward the mountains) are both worth the trip alone.

Worth knowing: The drive from town is short but the course is up a winding road through forest. Allow extra time. The clubhouse food is decent but unremarkable; eat in town instead.

Best For: The marquee round of your trip, mountain golf experience, when you want the best McCall has.

The Experience: McCall Golf Club has been the heart of the local golf scene for decades. Three nine-hole loops (Aspen, Birch, and Cedar) play in different combinations depending on what's open and what you want. The course is tree-lined parkland golf with mountain views — gentler than Jug Mountain Ranch but still beautiful. Slope 124 is moderate. Walking is encouraged and the layouts are pedestrian-friendly. Conditioning is good. The course sits right in town near Payette Lake, which means you can finish a morning round and walk to lunch downtown.

Best For: Walking rounds, in-town convenience, classic resort golf, couples or casual rounds, anyone who values playing 18 (or 27!) over playing the most dramatic course.

The Experience: Built in the 1980s and showing its age, but in a charming way. MeadowCreek is forgiving, friendly, and significantly cheaper than the marquee courses. Slope 117 means recreational golfers can actually post good scores here. The setting is pretty — meadows and creeks with mountain backdrops — even if the course design isn't world-class. Good pace, friendly staff, locals' atmosphere. The drive from McCall (15 minutes west to New Meadows) is part of the experience.

Best For: Budget rounds, beginner-friendly play, when the marquee courses are booked, when you want to actually post a decent score.

Also Worth Knowing About

Whitetail Club (McCall) — Private. Tom Weiskopf signature design. Considered one of the best courses in Idaho but very limited public access. If you know a member, take advantage. Otherwise wave at it as you drive past Jug Mountain Ranch.

Tamarack Resort Golf Club (Donnelly, 30 min south of McCall) — Tom Weiskopf design at the Tamarack ski resort. Has gone through ownership changes and operational hiccups over the years; check current public access status before driving down. When open, it's a top-tier mountain experience.

Council Mountain Golf Course (Council, 45 min south on Highway 95) — Small 9-hole course, slope 105, $25–$35. Worth knowing if you're driving Highway 95 between Star and McCall and want to break up the trip.

The McCall Golf Trip Strategies

Strategy 1: The Star → McCall Weekend (the classic)

Friday afternoon: Drive Star to McCall (2 hours up Highway 55 — beautiful drive). Check in. Dinner downtown.

Saturday: Morning at Jug Mountain Ranch (the marquee). Lunch in town. Afternoon: walk along Payette Lake or rent a SUP/kayak.

Sunday: Morning at McCall Golf Club. Lunch. Drive home.

Total: 2 rounds in 2 days, perfect summer weekend escape.

Strategy 2: The Three-Course Long Weekend

Day 1: Drive up, late afternoon round at MeadowCreek (warm-up).

Day 2: Jug Mountain Ranch (the showcase).

Day 3: McCall Golf Club, then drive home.

3 rounds in 3 days, all McCall-area golf, around $200–$250 in green fees total.

Strategy 3: The Family Lake Trip with Golf Mornings

If you're bringing the family for a McCall lake vacation, plan one round per day (morning), then meet the family at Payette Lake or downtown for the rest of the day. Course choices: Jug Mountain Day 1 (the marquee), McCall GC Day 3 (walkable, near downtown). Skip MeadowCreek unless you have time for a third round.

Strategy 4: The Brundage Mountain Bike + Golf Combo

Brundage Mountain (just outside McCall) has world-class summer mountain biking. Many trips combine MTB and golf — bike Brundage in the morning, play golf in the afternoon. Jug Mountain Ranch is your best bet for an afternoon round because it has the latest tee times.

Where to Eat & Stay

Food

  • Shore Lodge (The Narrows) — The iconic upscale dinner. Lakeside, special-occasion vibe. Reservations essential.
  • Pancake House (downtown McCall) — Breakfast institution. Lines on weekends. Worth them.
  • Toll Station Pizza (downtown) — Casual pizza, beer, post-round dinner
  • Salmon River Brewery (downtown) — McCall's craft brewery, decent pub food, the locals' hangout
  • Lardo's Grill & Saloon (downtown) — Steaks, burgers, classic American dinner
  • Ruperts at Hotel McCall — Upscale, less stuffy than Shore Lodge, downtown

Lodging

  • Shore Lodge — The marquee resort on Payette Lake. Premium price, premium location, full-service.
  • Hotel McCall — Historic boutique hotel downtown. Walking distance to everything. Mid-range price.
  • The Whitetail Club — Private members and guests only. Don't even check rates.
  • VRBO/Airbnb cabins — McCall has thousands. Cabins on the lake, in town, or in the surrounding forests. Best value for groups.
  • Brundage Bungalows — Cabins near Brundage Mountain, good for golf+ski/MTB combo trips

Pro Tips

  • Book Jug Mountain Ranch in advance for July and August. Peak summer weekends sell out 3–4 weeks ahead.
  • The drive from Star is part of the trip. Highway 55 along the Payette River is genuinely scenic. Plan to stop at the river at Banks for a break or a swim.
  • Elevation matters. 5,000 feet means the ball flies 8–10% farther than Boise/Star. Club down — what's normally a 7-iron becomes an 8-iron.
  • Mornings are cold even in July. 45°F at 7 AM is normal. Pack a fleece. By 10 AM you'll be in shorts.
  • Mosquitoes after rain. McCall can have biblical mosquito clouds in early summer after wet springs. Bring spray.
  • Walk McCall Golf Club. The course is walking-friendly and the lake views are better at walking pace than from a cart.
  • Combine with a day at Payette Lake. McCall is one of Idaho's best lakes — boat rentals, swimming, paddleboarding. Plan one non-golf day to enjoy it.
  • Tamarack is hit or miss. Check current operational status before driving down — the resort has been on-and-off over the years.
  • Don't drive at night. Highway 55 has frequent deer and elk crossings, especially dawn/dusk. Drive during daylight if possible.

Bottom Line

McCall is the closest place to play real mountain golf from Star. Two hours up Highway 55, three good courses, and a town built around lake-and-mountain summer recreation. Jug Mountain Ranch is your bucket-list round. McCall Golf Club is your walking, downtown round. MeadowCreek is your value option. Most Treasure Valley golfers should make this trip at least once a summer.

Book a cabin on Payette Lake, play 36 holes over two days, eat at Salmon River Brewery, swim in the lake. There aren't many summer weekends better than that.

Two hours from Star. World away in atmosphere.
