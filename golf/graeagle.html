Golf Courses Within 50 Miles of Graeagle, California ⛳

Sierra Nevada golf country — rated for a less-than-average golfer

How to Read This Guide

  • Slope Rating: Average is 113. Lower = more forgiving. Higher = more punishing. If you’re a higher-handicap golfer, look for slopes under 120.
  • Course Rating: The expected score for a scratch golfer — lower means easier.
  • Our Rating: ⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — how much fun a less-than-average golfer will have here (factoring in difficulty, pace, price, scenery, and ego damage).
  • Note: Mountain courses play differently — altitude adds distance to your shots, and elevation changes can be tricky. The ball flies farther up here!

3. Whitehawk Ranch Golf Club ⭐⭐⭐⭐

The crown jewel — worth the splurge

Detail Info
Location Clio, CA (just south of Graeagle)
Distance 5 miles
Type Public
Holes / Par 18 / 71
Yards 6,941 (tips)
Course Rating 72.0–72.6
Slope 128–136
Green Fees ~$120–$149
Phone (530) 836-0394

Why it’s great for you: Rated Top 10 in California. Dick Bailey design carved from the Mohawk Valley with seven streams, native grasses, California poppies, and quaking aspens. The reviews say it’s “harder than the rating suggests,” but fairways are generous and greens aren’t too undulating. The slope is steep from the tips (136) but much more forgiving from the forward tees. Outstanding practice facility. Treat yourself day — play from the whites.

TOP PICKS — Quick Reference

Best for a Less-Than-Average Golfer (ranked)

  1. Feather River Park Resort — Lowest slope (104), cheap, zero stress ($44)
  2. Graeagle Meadows — Best all-around value in the area ($45–$55, slope 119)
  3. Ponderosa (Truckee) — Cheapest golf around ($18–$32, slope 113)
  4. Bailey Creek (Lake Almanor) — User-friendly championship course (slope 126)
  5. Whitehawk Ranch — Premium but generous fairways (slope 128 from whites)

Best Value

  1. Ponderosa — $18 for 9 holes, $13.50 seniors!
  2. Feather River Park Resort — $44 for mountain 9 holes
  3. Graeagle Meadows — $45 weekday for 18 championship holes

Courses to Avoid Unless You’re Good

  • Grizzly Ranch — Slope 140, $175
  • Old Greenwood — Slope 140, Jack Nicklaus beast
  • Gray’s Crossing — Slope 140, $230, limited access
  • Coyote Moon — Slope 140, up to $225
  • Nakoma (The Dragon) — Slope 139, FLW clubhouse is the draw

All green fees are approximate and subject to change. Mountain courses are seasonal (generally May–October). Call ahead for current rates and conditions.
