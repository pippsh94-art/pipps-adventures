Golf Courses Within 50 Miles of the Bay Area ⛳

San Francisco Bay golf country — rated for a less-than-average golfer

How to Read This Guide

  • Slope Rating: Average is 113. Lower = more forgiving. Higher = more punishing. If you’re a higher-handicap golfer, look for slopes under 120.
  • Course Rating: The expected score for a scratch golfer — lower means easier.
  • Our Rating: ⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — how much fun a less-than-average golfer will have here (factoring in difficulty, pace, price, wind, and ego damage).
  • Note: Bay Area golf means wind. Afternoon breezes can add 2+ clubs, especially on links-style courses near the water. Dress in layers — Karl the Fog doesn’t care about your tee time.

SAN FRANCISCO (0–10 Miles)

1. Golden Gate Park Golf Course ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The most fun you’ll have losing zero balls

Detail Info
Location 970 47th Ave, San Francisco, CA
Type Public (9 holes)
Holes / Par 9 / 27 (par-3)
Yards 1,357
Slope 80
Green Fees ~$45–$55 (9 holes)
Phone (415) 751-8987

Why it’s great for you: Slope of 80 — the lowest in the Bay Area. Recently redesigned by Jay Blasi with firm fescue, wild greens, and ocean-adjacent vibes. A short course that’s deceptively fun — the bounces and contours reward creativity. New clubhouse and outdoor seating make it a scene. Walk-only. Best “zero stress” option in SF.

Detail Info

Yards ~5,700 (18-hole play)
Slope ~115

TOP PICKS — Quick Reference

Best for a Less-Than-Average Golfer (ranked)

  1. Golden Gate Park — Slope 80, par-3, firm fescue fun (~$45–$55)
  2. Lincoln Park — Slope 109, Golden Gate Bridge views ($48–$62)
  3. Poplar Creek (San Mateo) — Slope 115, flat and forgiving (~$50–$60)
  4. Mare Island (Vallejo) — Slope 115, budget-friendly (~$40–$65)
  5. Diablo Creek (Concord) — Slope 118, best casual East Bay course (~$45–$66)

Best Value

  1. Lake Chabot Par-3 — $13 for 9 holes (Oakland)
  2. Gilroy — $26 for a wild 18-hole adventure
  3. Golden Gate Park — $45 for the most fun 9 holes in SF
  4. Lincoln Park — $48 SF resident for 18 holes with views
  5. Mare Island — $40 for a full 18 in Vallejo

Best “Treat Yourself” Courses

  1. Half Moon Bay — Old Course — Clifftop ocean golf ($225–$275)
  2. Presidio — Historic SF landmark ($130–$150)
  3. TPC Harding Park — PGA Tour venue ($40–$120)
  4. Cinnabar Hills — South Bay’s premier 27 holes ($100–$150)
  5. Poppy Ridge — Wine country links ($75–$125 NCGA)

Courses to Avoid Unless You’re Good

  • Hiddenbrooke — Slope 142, Arnold Palmer beast
  • Poppy Ridge — Slope 141, long and wind-swept
  • Presidio — Slope 136, tight and pricey
  • Cinnabar Hills (Mountain) — Slope 135, elevation changes
  • Boundary Oak — Slope 132, 7,000+ yards

All green fees are approximate and subject to change. Bay Area courses often use dynamic pricing — book online for best rates. SF Resident cards unlock major discounts at city courses. NCGA membership ($80/year) saves big at Poppy Ridge and other NorCal courses.
