Golf Courses Within 50 Miles of Sacramento, California ⛳

Capital City golf country — rated for a less-than-average golfer

How to Read This Guide

  • Slope Rating: Average is 113. Lower = more forgiving. Higher = more punishing. If you’re a higher-handicap golfer, look for slopes under 120.
  • Course Rating: The expected score for a scratch golfer — lower means easier.
  • Our Rating: ⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — how much fun a less-than-average golfer will have here (factoring in difficulty, pace, price, and ego damage).
  • Note: Sacramento’s Central Valley heat means summer rounds start early. Most courses are year-round, which is a huge perk over the mountains.

TOP PICKS — Quick Reference

Best for a Less-Than-Average Golfer (ranked)

  1. Cordova Golf Course — Lowest slope (92), executive length, cheap (~$30–$44)
  2. William Land Golf Course — Short 9-holer, slope 113, under $25
  3. Haggin Oaks (Arcade Creek) — Best full-length value, slope 120 ($32–$48)
  4. Diamond Oaks (Roseville) — Friendly classic, slope 118 (~$49–$65)
  5. Timber Creek (Roseville) — Fan favorite, slope 124, great conditions

Best Value

  1. William Land — Under $25 for 9 holes
  2. Bing Maloney / Haggin Oaks (Arcade Creek) — Under $50 for 18 holes
  3. Cordova — 18 executive holes for ~$30–$44
  4. Bartley Cavanaugh — Muni pricing, river views
  5. Cherry Island — County rates, less crowded

Best “Treat Yourself” Courses

  1. Yocha Dehe — Troon luxury ($175–$325)
  2. DarkHorse — Golf Digest Top 10 ($79–$89)
  3. Ancil Hoffman — Best-conditioned public course ($45–$65)
  4. Haggin Oaks (MacKenzie) — Play where MacKenzie designed ($40–$62)

Courses to Avoid Unless You’re Good

  • The Ridge — Slope 142, ravines everywhere
  • DarkHorse — Slope 140, 7,200 yards
  • Whitney Oaks — Slope 140, forced carries
  • Turkey Creek — Slope 136, championship length
  • Morgan Creek — Slope 135, water everywhere

All green fees are approximate and subject to change. Call ahead or check online for current rates, twilight specials, and senior discounts. Green fees generally don’t include cart unless noted. Most courses offer GolfNow/TeeOff booking discounts.
