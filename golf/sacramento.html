Golf Courses Within 50 Miles of Sacramento, California ⛳

A less-than-average golfer's guide to the Central Valley — rated for fun, forgiveness, value, and brutal honesty

Why This Guide Exists

Sacramento gets slept on. While everyone's obsessing over Napa wine country and the Bay Area's expensive coastal courses, the Central Valley has 25+ solid public courses, crazy-cheap rates, and year-round golf. You can play a championship course for the price of lunch.

But the Valley has real character: crushing summer heat (play before 8am June-August), flat agricultural landscape that makes some courses feel repetitive, and a schedule that demands smarter play. This guide is honest about what works and what'll make you hate golf.

The ratings focus on what matters to a recreational golfer: Can you actually play here? Is the difficulty reasonable? Is it worth the drive? Are you paying Bay Area prices for Valley golf? Skip the hype. Play the good ones.

How to Read This Guide

  • Slope Rating: Average is 113. Lower = more forgiving. Higher = more punishing. Under 120 is manageable for high-handicappers. 130+ will humble you. Sacramento's municipal courses cluster in the 120-125 range — friendly by championship standards.
  • Course Rating: Expected score for a scratch golfer. Less useful for you, but courses publish it.
  • Our Rating: ⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — how much fun you'll actually have (factoring in difficulty, pace, price, and ego damage).
  • Sacramento's Geography: Downtown Sacramento sits between the American River (north) and the Sacramento River (south/east). Courses spread in three clusters: city center (0-8 miles), east/northeast (8-20 miles), and foothills/burbs (20-50 miles).

Sacramento Golf: What You Need to Know

The Heat

Central Valley summers are no joke — 95-105°F is normal June-August. Courses open at 6am and rounds book solid. Play early or you'll bake. Winter is incredible (60-70°F, sunny). Fall (September-November) is actually better than Bay Area golf. Spring can be windy (March-May), but manageable.

The Season

Best: October-May. Mild, playable every day, perfect light. Winter mornings are cold (40s), but afternoon is beautiful.

Worst: June-August. Early tee times only (6-8am). Afternoon is suicide.

Summer Advantage: Unlike mountain courses, Sacramento courses stay open 365 days. While you're snowed out up north, Sacramento's playing.

The Landscape & Course Character

Central Valley courses are flat or gently rolling — no elevation drama. Expect tree-lined fair-way courses (oaks, cottonwoods) near the rivers, more open/agricultural layouts outside the city. The flat terrain makes courses faster and walkable. No wind channel drama like the Bay Area, but summer afternoon thermals can gust.

The Value

Sacramento's biggest asset: cheap. Municipal courses run $30-$48 for 18 holes. County courses ($35-$55) punch above their weight. You're paying $12-$20 cart fees on top. Even at Haggin Oaks (Sacramento's signature course), $48 + $18 cart = $66 total. That's cheaper than a Bay Area muni.

Cart vs. Walking

Most Sacramento courses require carts ($15-$20). William Land and some 9-holers are walkable. If you want to walk, call ahead.

AVOID UNLESS YOU'RE GOOD

These courses are beautiful and challenging. Unless you're a decent golfer, you'll lose a lot of balls, take 5+ hours, and feel bad about yourself. Play forward tees or skip entirely.

  • The Ridge — Slope 142, ravines, forced carries, elevation
  • DarkHorse — Slope 139–140, 7,200 yards, championship beast
  • Whitney Oaks — Slope 132–140, forced carries, ravines
  • Turkey Creek — Slope 136, championship length, no mercy
  • Morgan Creek — Slope 135, water everywhere, inconsistent conditions
  • Teal Bend — Slope 129–132, water on many holes, wetlands eat balls
  • Wildhorse (Davis) — Slope 134, links-style wind, no shelter

All green fees are approximate and subject to change. Call ahead or check online for current rates, twilight specials, and senior discounts. Green fees generally don't include cart unless noted. Most courses offer GolfNow/TeeOff booking discounts. Summer play: book 6-7am tee times or suffer heat.
