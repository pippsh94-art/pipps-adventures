Top 10 Golf Courses Across All Regions ⛳

The standouts from 100+ courses across 11 western US destinations — best scenery, best value, best challenge, best resort, and the bucket-list rounds you should play once

About These Rankings

This is the cross-cutting summary across the regional guides — Bay Area, Sacramento, Graeagle, Star Idaho, Coeur d'Alene, McCall, Sun Valley, Hurricane Utah, Mesquite Nevada, Reno-Tahoe, and Bend Oregon. Together those guides cover 100+ playable courses across the western US, ranging from $25 munis to $400 bucket-list rounds.

The categories below answer different questions a recreational golfer actually asks: Where's the most beautiful course? The best value? The hardest test? The one I should fly to play once? Each pick links to its full review in the regional guide.

This is opinionated, not objective. The rankings reflect a less-than-average golfer's perspective: prioritizing fun, forgiveness, scenery, and value over slope ratings and championship pedigree.

🗓️ Best by Season

Spring (April–May)

When it's still winter most places, these regions are at their best.

  • Hurricane, UT & Mesquite, NV — Peak season, perfect 60–75°F weather, low rates
  • Bay Area — Mild, less crowded than fall
  • Sacramento — Wildflowers, perfect temps before summer heat
  • Star, ID — Treasure Valley shoulder season, courses opening, great rates

Summer (June–August)

Mountain golf prime time. Avoid desert courses unless you have early tee times.

  • Sun Valley, ID — Peak alpine golf, perfect 75°F days
  • McCall, ID — Same — peak mountain summer
  • Coeur d'Alene, ID — Peak season, lake activities, premium prices
  • Lake Tahoe — Same
  • Bend, OR — Peak season, all courses fully open, 80°F days
  • Graeagle, CA — Sierra summer escape
  • Bay Area — Coastal courses are perfect, valley courses are hot

Fall (September–October)

The locals' favorite season everywhere. Conditions still excellent, crowds gone, rates dropping.

  • Bend, OR — September is perfect; the smartest month to visit
  • Sun Valley, ID — Aspens turn gold, locals' favorite
  • Coeur d'Alene, ID — Lake colors are stunning, rates drop 30–40%
  • Bay Area — The classic golf season — sunny, dry, perfect
  • Sacramento — Cooling off after summer heat

Winter (November–March)

When snow buries the Pacific Northwest and Idaho mountains, these regions become the only game in town.

  • Hurricane, UT & Mesquite, NV — Peak season for snowbirds, book months ahead
  • Star, ID — Treasure Valley plays year-round (cold mornings but no snow most years)
  • Bay Area — Mild winters, fewer crowds, lowest rates
  • Sacramento — Same — winter is value season

🎁 The Hidden Gems

Underrated courses that don't get the press but deliver outsized experiences.

  • RedHawk (Treasure Valley) — $24–$29 for a championship lake-view course. The best value in this entire guide.
  • Brasada Canyons (Bend) — Hidden marquee. Cascades from every hole. Quieter than Pronghorn, equally beautiful.
  • Dixie Red Hills (St. George) — Old-school city course with massive red rock cliffs as backdrop. Walkable, $39–$55. Atmosphere you can't pay for.
  • Genoa Lakes (Genoa, NV) — Two courses in the Carson Valley between Reno and Tahoe. Most golfers drive past without stopping.
  • Aspen Lakes (Sisters, OR) — The famous red-cinder bunkers. Slope 128 at $69–$99.
  • Avondale Golf Club (Hayden Lake, ID) — 1932 parkland course you can't build anymore. The locals' choice in the CdA area.
  • Jug Mountain Ranch (McCall, ID) — Slope 138 mountain layout at $85–$130. Star's best escape.

🚫 Skip List

Courses that get hyped but don't deliver value, or are just not worth the trip given the alternatives.

  • Vegas Strip golf courses ($200–$400+) — Skip them and drive to Mesquite. You'll play better courses for less money.
  • Wolf Creek if you're not a low handicapper — Slope 153 will break your spirit. The views aren't worth the suffering.
  • Most "designed by [celebrity]" celebrity-licensed courses — Branding, not design. The actual designer is usually an associate.
  • Old Russian Olive trees and tamarisk landscaping — Avoid courses that incorporate these invasive species into landscaping; it's a sign of cheap maintenance.

  2. Pronghorn Nicklaus — Top-100 public, Cascades, Nicklaus design.

The Two Trips Every Western Golfer Should Take Once

1. The Idaho Triangle

Star → McCall → Sun Valley → Coeur d'Alene → home. 7 days, all in Idaho, all of the state's best golf in a single trip. Drive Star → McCall (2 hours, 1 round at Jug Mountain Ranch). Drive McCall → Sun Valley via Stanley (4 hours, scenic, 2 rounds at Trail Creek and BIGWOOD). Drive Sun Valley → Coeur d'Alene (long, ~7 hours through Salmon, 2 rounds at the Resort and Circling Raven). Drive home (5 hours). Yes, the driving is significant, but the variety of Idaho golf in one trip is unmatched.

2. The Pacific Northwest Loop

Bend → Tahoe → Graeagle → Reno → home. 7 days, premium mountain and high-desert golf. Fly into Redmond, OR. Play Pronghorn, Tetherow, and Crosswater over 3 days. Drive Bend → Lake Tahoe (6.5 hours through Burns and Reno — long but scenic). Play Edgewood and Coyote Moon over 2 days. Drive Tahoe → Graeagle (1 hour). Play Plumas Pines and Graeagle Meadows over 2 days. Fly out of Reno. The most concentrated stretch of premium western mountain golf you can fit in a week.

Final Thoughts

Across 100+ courses spread over 11 western US destinations, the recurring lesson is that place matters more than design pedigree. The best rounds aren't the ones with the highest slope ratings or the most famous architects — they're the ones where the setting, the conditioning, the pace, and the company come together. Coeur d'Alene's Resort course is famous for the floating green, but the experience of forecaddies, perfect conditioning, and lakeside scenery is what makes it worth the price. Sun Valley's Trail Creek is famous for Hemingway, but it's the heritage and the alpine setting that make it essential.

If you only have time for a few rounds, prioritize the experiences you can't get elsewhere: the floating green, the lakeside championship, the inland links, the historic resort, the canyon view. Save the casual rounds for your home courses.

Golf is a lifetime sport. There's plenty of time to play them all.

Pick a course. Pick a date. Make the trip. The rest takes care of itself.
