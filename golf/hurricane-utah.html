Golf Courses Near Hurricane, Utah ⛳

Red rock golf in the high desert — stunning views, wide open fairways, year-round play

Why This Guide Exists

Hurricane (pronounced "HUR-kin") is the gateway to southwest Utah golf. It's 15 minutes from St. George, 45 minutes from Zion National Park. If you're visiting Zion or doing a Utah road trip, these courses are perfect for a golf break between hiking. The red rock scenery is unmatched. Winter is actually peak season here (while everywhere else is snowed out).

The courses range from forgiving valley layouts to challenging resort designs. Most importantly: you'll have nearly perfect weather March-May and September-November. Summer is hot (95-100°F) but playable with early tee times. Winter is ideal (50-70°F).

Hurricane / St. George Golf: What You Need to Know

The Season & Weather

Best: October-April. Perfect golf weather — 50-75°F, sunny, minimal wind. This is why people escape to St. George in winter.

Summer: May-September is hot (85-105°F) but still playable. Play early (before 8am) or skip it.

Wind: Spring (March-May) has afternoon breezes. Manageable but noticeable. Afternoon tee times = 1-2 clubs longer.

The Landscape

High desert golf at 2,600-3,000 ft elevation. Red rock bluffs, some water features (mostly in resort courses), wide fairways. Very different from California golf — the light is golden, shadows are dramatic, and the views are genuinely stunning. Ball carries 5-10% farther than sea level.

The Vibe

This is resort golf country. Most courses are designed for vacationers and part-time residents. Pace is relaxed. Prices are fair (not cheap, not expensive). Everyone's here to enjoy themselves and the scenery.

Where to Stay

Hurricane is small (15K people). St. George (10 miles) has hotels and restaurants. If you're golfing multiple days, Zion escarpment views beat any clubhouse.

Also Worth Knowing About

Green Spring Golf Course (Washington, just south) — Slope 115, $39-59. Very forgiving, great for beginners or when you just want to have fun. 9-hole option too.

Sand Hollow Golf Course (Toquerville, 20 miles northeast toward Zion) — Slope 135, $99-129. Beautiful desert layout, harder challenge. Great if you're staying near Zion.

Equestrian Golf Club (St. George area) — Resort private course. May have limited public play — call ahead.

The Zion Golf Weekend Strategy

Friday: Arrive St. George. Play Southgate (afternoon/twilight). Dinner in Hurricane.

Saturday: Morning: Entrada at Snow Canyon (treat yourself). Afternoon: Quick hike at Snow Canyon State Park (literally next door). Evening: Sunset stroll in downtown St. George.

Sunday: Morning: Early tee time at LaQuinta (quick round). By noon: 45 minutes to Zion National Park. Afternoon/evening: Angels Landing or other hikes.

This works because the courses are clustered and Zion is 45 minutes away. You get world-class golf AND world-class hiking on the same trip.

Pro Tips

  • Book twilight rounds. Save $20-30, play in perfect light, avoid afternoon heat. 2pm tee times are ideal in winter/spring.
  • Winter is peak season. January-February is when all the snowbirds arrive. Book in advance. But the weather is perfect.
  • Bring sunscreen. Red rock reflects UV like crazy. Desert sun is no joke even at 60°F.
  • Elevation affects your game. You'll hit the ball 5-10% farther than sea level. Club selection matters. Test on the range.
  • Play forward tees if slopes are 130+. Sky Mountain and Entrada are designed to challenge. No shame going up two tees.
  • Cash discounts exist. Call ahead for locals' rates or package deals, especially Oct-April.

Bottom Line

Hurricane/St. George is perfect for a Zion trip golf break. You get stunning red rock scenery, solid courses, year-round weather, and zero crowds (compared to California). Entrada is your best course. Southgate is your best value + views combo. LaQuinta is your quick warm-up. Winter is when you should go.

This is golf where the scenery is half the experience. Play for the game, but stay for the views.
