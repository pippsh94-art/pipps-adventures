Golf in Hurricane, Utah ⛳

Red rock golf in the high desert — stunning views, wide open fairways, year-round play

Why This Guide Exists

Hurricane (pronounced "HUR-kin") is the gateway to southwest Utah golf. It's 15 minutes from St. George, 45 minutes from Zion National Park. If you're visiting Zion or doing a Utah road trip, these courses are perfect for a golf break between hiking. The red rock scenery is unmatched. Winter is actually peak season here (while everywhere else is snowed out).

The courses range from forgiving valley layouts to challenging resort designs. Most importantly: you'll have nearly perfect weather March-May and September-November. Summer is hot (95-100°F) but playable with early tee times. Winter is ideal (50-70°F).

Hurricane / St. George Golf: What You Need to Know

The Season & Weather

Best: October-April. Perfect golf weather — 50-75°F, sunny, minimal wind. This is why people escape to St. George in winter.

Summer: May-September is hot (85-105°F) but still playable. Play early (before 8am) or skip it.

Wind: Spring (March-May) has afternoon breezes. Manageable but noticeable. Afternoon tee times = 1-2 clubs longer.

The Landscape

High desert golf at 2,600-3,000 ft elevation. Red rock bluffs, some water features (mostly in resort courses), wide fairways. Very different from California golf — the light is golden, shadows are dramatic, and the views are genuinely stunning. Ball carries 5-10% farther than sea level.

The Vibe

This is resort golf country. Most courses are designed for vacationers and part-time residents. Pace is relaxed. Prices are fair (not cheap, not expensive). Everyone's here to enjoy themselves and the scenery.

Where to Stay

Hurricane is small (15K people). St. George (10 miles) has hotels and restaurants. If you're golfing multiple days, Zion escarpment views beat any clubhouse.

Also Worth Knowing About

Sand Hollow Golf Course (Hurricane area, on the way to Zion) — Slope 135, $99–129. Beautiful Bill Coore-influenced desert layout with red rock framing nearly every hole. Tougher than Southgate but a bucket-list course for many. Worth it if you're playing more than 2 rounds in the area.

Green Spring Golf Course (Washington, 10 miles south) — Slope 115, $39–59. Very forgiving public course, great for beginners or quick warm-up rounds. 9-hole option available. Often the cheapest sane round in the area.

Dixie Red Hills Golf Course (St. George) — Old-school city course with massive red rock cliffs as backdrop. Slope 119, $39–55. Walkable, atmospheric, and the views are unreal for the price. Hidden gem.

St. George Golf Club (St. George) — Slope 124, $49–69. Solid municipal-style course, good conditioning, reasonable rates. The locals' choice for quick weekday rounds.

Coral Canyon Golf Course (Washington) — Slope 130, $59–89. Mature 1990s desert layout with water features. Decent option when the bigger names are booked.

Equestrian Estates Golf Club (St. George area) — Mostly private. Limited public play windows. Call ahead.

The Zion Golf Weekend Strategies

Strategy 1: The Classic 3-Day (Golf + Zion)

Friday: Arrive St. George/Hurricane area. Play Southgate (afternoon/twilight rate). Dinner in Hurricane or downtown St. George.

Saturday: Morning: Entrada at Snow Canyon (treat yourself, the marquee round). Afternoon: Quick hike at Snow Canyon State Park (literally next door). Evening: Sunset at Pioneer Park or downtown St. George.

Sunday: Early tee time at LaQuinta or Green Spring (quick round). By noon: 45 minutes to Zion National Park. Afternoon: Angels Landing or The Narrows.

Strategy 2: The Pure Golf Weekend (No Hiking)

Friday: Southgate (afternoon).

Saturday: Morning at Sand Hollow (the bucket list round). Afternoon at LaQuinta or Sky Mountain (depending on energy).

Sunday: Morning at Entrada (best course of the trip — save it for the freshest legs). Done by noon, drive home.

Total: 4 rounds in 3 days. Pace: aggressive but doable in 60–70°F winter weather.

Strategy 3: The Couples Getaway

Day 1: Arrive, play Sunbrook (most walkable, scenic, moderate difficulty). Dinner in St. George.

Day 2: Morning: Southgate together. Afternoon: Snow Canyon State Park or downtown St. George shopping.

Day 3: Drive into Zion for the day. Hike Riverside Walk or Emerald Pools (no permits needed).

Strategy 4: The Phoenix-to-Boise Road Trip Stop

If you're driving I-15 between Phoenix and Boise, the Hurricane/St. George cluster is exactly the right "break the trip" spot — about 7 hours from each. One night, one round at Southgate or LaQuinta, eat dinner at George's Corner Restaurant, sleep, drive on.

  • Holiday Inn Express St. George — Budget-friendly, central, plenty of options nearby

  • Zion Lodge (inside Zion NP) — Only if you want to be in the park; book months ahead

Pro Tips

  • Book twilight rounds. Save $20-30, play in perfect light, avoid afternoon heat. 2pm tee times are ideal in winter/spring.
  • Winter is peak season. January-February is when all the snowbirds arrive. Book in advance. But the weather is perfect.
  • Bring sunscreen. Red rock reflects UV like crazy. Desert sun is no joke even at 60°F.
  • Elevation affects your game. You'll hit the ball 5-10% farther than sea level. Club selection matters. Test on the range.
  • Play forward tees if slopes are 130+. Sky Mountain and Entrada are designed to challenge. No shame going up two tees.
  • Cash discounts exist. Call ahead for locals' rates or package deals, especially Oct-April.
  • Go midweek if possible. Tuesday–Thursday tee times in peak season are 20–30% cheaper than weekend rates and the courses are noticeably less crowded.
  • Pair Sand Hollow with Zion. If you're doing the bucket-list round at Sand Hollow, do it the morning before a Zion afternoon. They're 30 minutes apart and the contrast (red rock golf, then red rock hiking) is unforgettable.
  • Skip the resort packages on Entrada. Public play on Entrada is allowed but rates jump if you book through a resort partner. Call the course directly for the best price.
  • Drink water like it's your job. The dry desert air pulls moisture out of you faster than you realize. A 60°F day in St. George dehydrates like a 90°F day in California.

Bottom Line

Hurricane/St. George is perfect for a Zion trip golf break. You get stunning red rock scenery, solid courses, year-round weather, and zero crowds (compared to California). Entrada is your best course. Southgate is your best value + views combo. LaQuinta is your quick warm-up. Winter is when you should go.

This is golf where the scenery is half the experience. Play for the game, but stay for the views.
