# Return-Loop Options: Glacier/Banff → Star, ID

Instead of retracing US-93 / I-90 / US-95, turn the trip into a circle. Three loops below — all trailer-friendly with notes on passes to avoid.

**Rig reminder:** F150 + 16' Lance. Watch grades, pass heights, and campground length limits.

---

## Option A: Yellowstone + Grand Teton (~5 extra days, +~700 mi)

The legendary loop. Adds two crown-jewel parks on the way home.

### Route (from Glacier/Waterton side)

1. **Glacier/Waterton → Great Falls, MT** via I-15 S or US-89 S (scenic, through Browning/Choteau along the Rocky Mountain Front). ~3 hrs.
2. **Great Falls → Bozeman** on I-15 S → US-287 S through Townsend/Three Forks. ~3 hrs, easy towing.
3. **Bozeman → Gardiner (North entrance)** via US-89 S through Paradise Valley. 1.5 hrs, gorgeous.
4. **Exit south via John D. Rockefeller Pkwy** into **Grand Teton** (Colter Bay area).
5. **Tetons → Idaho Falls** via US-26 W through **Swan Valley** (trailer-friendly — **avoid Teton Pass on WY-22, 10% grades not worth the risk**).
6. **Idaho Falls → Star** via I-15 → I-84 W. ~4 hrs.

### Where to Camp

| Park | Campground | Trailer fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Yellowstone | **Madison** | Up to 40' | Central to Old Faithful + Norris. Reservable via recreation.gov. |
| Yellowstone | **Canyon** | Up to 40' | Best base for Canyon + Lamar Valley. Flush toilets, showers. |
| Yellowstone | **Fishing Bridge RV Park** | Full hookups | Only hookup site in park. Books out 13 months early. |
| Yellowstone | **Grant Village** | Up to 40' | Base for West Thumb + south loop to Tetons. |
| Grand Teton | **Colter Bay RV Park** | Full hookups | Best base, near Jackson Lake. Reservable. |
| Grand Teton | **Signal Mountain** | Up to 30' (tight) | Iconic lake views. First-come + some reservable. |
| Grand Teton | **Gros Ventre** | Up to 45' | Huge, easy, close to Jackson. |

### Priority Stops

**Yellowstone (3 nights min):**
1. **Old Faithful + Upper Geyser Basin** — Grand Prismatic overlook from Fairy Falls trail is the real shot.
2. **Lamar Valley at dawn or dusk** — wolves, bison, bears. "America's Serengeti."
3. **Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone** — Artist Point, Uncle Tom's Trail, Brink of Lower Falls.
4. **Norris Geyser Basin** — hottest, most active.
5. **Mammoth Hot Springs** — travertine terraces; elk often in town.

**Grand Teton (2 nights min):**
1. **Jenny Lake** — boat shuttle + Cascade Canyon / Hidden Falls (easy-moderate).
2. **Schwabacher Landing** at sunrise — the postcard shot.
3. **Mormon Row** — barns with Tetons behind.
4. **Signal Mountain Summit Rd** — 360° view (trailer-OK road).
5. **Oxbow Bend** — moose at dawn.
6. **Jackson, WY** — dinner/supplies stop, antler arches on the square.

| Day | Plan |
|---|---|
| +1 | Banff/Glacier exit → Great Falls or Bozeman (long drive) |
| +2 | → Gardiner, set up Canyon or Madison. Afternoon Mammoth. |
| +3 | Old Faithful + Grand Prismatic + Lamar sunset |
| +4 | Canyon of the Yellowstone → move to Colter Bay |
| +5 | Jenny Lake + Schwabacher + drive south to Idaho Falls |
| +6 | Idaho Falls → Star |

## Option B: Sawtooths via Salmon River (~3 extra days, +~200 mi)

Way less crowded than Yellowstone. Gorgeous, and technically "on the way" if you come down US-93.

### Route

1. **Missoula → Salmon, ID** via US-93 S over **Lost Trail Pass** (7,014', graded, truck lanes). 2.5 hrs.
2. **Salmon → Stanley** via ID-75 through Sunbeam along the Salmon River. 2 hrs, scenic canyon driving, trailer-fine.
3. **Stanley → Ketchum** via ID-75 over **Galena Summit** (8,701', 7% grade — the only real concern of the loop; take it slow, use engine braking). 1 hr.
4. **Ketchum → Star** via US-20 W through Fairfield → I-84 W. 3 hrs.

**Skip:** ID-21 from Stanley to Lowman (Banner Summit, steep + narrow, sketchy with 16' trailer).

### Where to Camp

| Campground | Trailer fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Redfish Lake** (Glacier View, Outlet, Point) | Up to 35' | Crown jewel. Reservable via recreation.gov, books fast. |
| **Stanley Lake** (Inlet, Lake View) | Up to 32' | Quieter than Redfish, McGown Peak reflections. |
| **Mormon Bend** (on Salmon River) | Up to 30' | Between Stanley and Sunbeam, easy riverfront. |
| **Sunny Gulch** | Up to 45' | Largest near Stanley, easy in/out. |
| **Easley** (on Big Wood near Galena) | Up to 30' | Between Stanley and Ketchum. |

### Priority Stops

1. **Redfish Lake** — rent a kayak/SUP, boat shuttle to Transfer Camp for Redfish Lake Creek hike.
2. **Sawtooth Lake hike** — 10 mi RT from Iron Creek TH, the iconic Sawtooth photo.
3. **Alice Lake / Toxaway Loop** — epic if you have a layover day (day hike to Alice is 11 mi RT).
4. **Stanley Lake + McGown Peak** at sunset.
5. **Salmon River hot springs** — Sunbeam, Bonneville, Kem (free, primitive, varying driving access).
6. **Ketchum / Sun Valley** — Sawtooth Brewery, Warm Springs lift-served hikes.
7. **Galena Summit overlook** — pull-off at the top; whole Sawtooth range spread below.

### 3-Day Add-On Sketch

| Day | Plan |
|---|---|
| +1 | Missoula → Stanley via Salmon. Set up Redfish or Stanley Lake. |
| +2 | Sawtooth Lake or Alice Lake hike; evening on Redfish |
| +3 | Stanley → Ketchum → Star |

## Option C: Craters of the Moon Dip (~1 extra day, +~80 mi)

Quick add-on if coming south via Idaho Falls (works with Option A, or as a solo detour).

### Route

- From **Idaho Falls**, take US-20 W to **Arco** (pass the EBR-1 atomic museum if it's open).
- Enter **Craters of the Moon NM** — otherworldly volcanic landscape.
- Continue US-20/26 W to Mountain Home → I-84 → Star.

### Camping + Stops

- **Lava Flow Campground** (in the monument) — up to 32', first-come, no hookups, no shade (hot in summer, so plan for shoulder season).
- **Inferno Cone** — 10-min walk, moonscape views. Apollo astronauts trained here.
- **Cave Area** — Indian Tunnel and Dewdrop Cave (free permit at visitor center).
- **North Crater Flow Trail** — short loop through fresh-looking lava.

Half a day is enough if you're not camping. A night in Lava Flow is memorable just for the dark sky.

## Combined Mega-Loop (all three, ~7 extra days)

Banff/Yoho → Great Falls → Yellowstone → Grand Teton → Idaho Falls → Craters of the Moon → Sun Valley → Sawtooths → Star. ~25 days total from home. Ambitious but the Rockies in one go.

**Only do this if:** You have the time, your rig is dialed, and you're not getting trip fatigue. Probably better as two separate years.

## Booking Heads-Up

- **Yellowstone/Grand Teton CGs:** recreation.gov, 6 months out to the day, set an alarm.
- **Fishing Bridge RV:** 13 months out, books in minutes.
- **Sawtooths (Redfish):** 6 months out, weekends go first.
- **Craters of the Moon:** first-come, usually fine outside peak summer weekends.
