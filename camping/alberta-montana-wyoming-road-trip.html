1. Overview & Route Map

This is a full loop from Star, Idaho — roughly 1,800 miles of driving not counting in-park miles. You'll cross into Canada and back once each way, linking the best national parks in western Canada with Glacier, Yellowstone, and Grand Teton.

The Loop at a Glance

LegDriveApprox Time
Star → Glacier NP (west entrance)~330 mi via Missoula5.5 hrs
Glacier NP → Waterton Lakes (border)~70 mi via Chief Mountain1.5 hrs
Waterton → Calgary~165 mi north on AB-22.5 hrs
Calgary → Banff → Yoho → Icefields → Jasper~280 mi (all-day stops)2–3 days driving
Jasper → Calgary (return south)~230 mi via AB-93/AB-24 hrs
Calgary → US Border (Coutts/Sweetgrass)~200 mi south on AB-23 hrs
Sweetgrass → Yellowstone (North Gate)~290 mi via Bozeman4.5 hrs
Yellowstone → Grand Teton~60 mi south1 hr
Grand Teton → Star, ID~230 mi via Alpine/Swan Valley3.5 hrs

Route Order (Counter-clockwise loop)

  1. Star, ID → north through Idaho/Montana → Missoula → Glacier NP
  2. Glacier NP → east side → Chief Mountain border → Waterton Lakes
  3. Waterton → north to Calgary (supply stop)
  4. Calgary → west to Banff → Yoho → Icefields Parkway → Jasper
  5. Jasper → south back through Alberta → cross US border at Sweetgrass/Coutts
  6. Great Falls → Bozeman → Livingston → Yellowstone North Entrance
  7. Yellowstone → Grand Teton → Jackson, WY → Swan Valley → Star, ID

Key Facts

  • Total driving: ~1,800 miles (not counting in-park miles)
  • Border crossings: 2 (Star→Canada via Chief Mountain; Canada→US via Sweetgrass/Coutts)
  • Best season: June–September
  • Minimum time: 3 weeks (intense) — 4 weeks is more comfortable
  • US National Parks Pass ($80): Covers Glacier, Yellowstone, and Grand Teton — buy it

2. 🚗 Leg 1: Star, ID → Glacier National Park (~5.5 hrs)

Head north out of Star on ID-16 to I-84, then pick up US-95 north through McCall or take I-84 east to I-15 north. The most scenic route goes through McCall and Salmon River country before connecting to US-93 through the Bitterroot Valley into Missoula, then north on US-93 to Glacier's west entrance.

Recommended Route (West Entrance)

Star → Boise → US-95 north → New Meadows → Grangeville → US-12 west → Lolo → Missoula → US-93 north → Kalispell → Glacier West Entrance (Apgar)

  • ~330 miles, 5.5 hours without stops
  • Stunning scenery through the Bitterroot Valley — nothing boring about this drive
  • Stop in Missoula for food/fuel (good college-town breweries and restaurants)
  • Kalispell is the last major city before the park — stock up on groceries and propane here

Alternative Route (East Entrance — adds the Going-to-the-Sun Road from east)

Star → I-84 → I-86 → I-15 north → Great Falls → US-89 north → Browning → Glacier East Entrance (St. Mary)

  • ~380 miles, 6 hrs
  • Less scenic through Idaho but opens up the stunning east side of Glacier
  • Lets you drive Going-to-the-Sun Road west to east

3. 📍 Glacier National Park, Montana

Glacier is often in the shadow of Yellowstone but it's every bit as spectacular — possibly more dramatic scenery. This is one of the most beautiful places in the United States.

Highlights

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road — 50 miles through the heart of the park over Logan Pass. One of the best drives in the US. ⚠️ Requires a vehicle reservation June 15–Labor Day — book early on recreation.gov
  • Lake McDonald — crystal clear lake with colorful painted pebbles; drive the west shore
  • Lake McDonald Lodge — historic 1914 lodge; take a ranger-led lake cruise
  • Avalanche Lake Trail — 4.5 mi round-trip through old-growth cedar to a glacial cirque lake
  • Trail of the Cedars — easy boardwalk, great if you want something quick
  • Grinnell Glacier Trail — one of the best hikes in the park; 11 mi round-trip, you see an actual glacier
  • Many Glacier area — east side; wildflower meadows, abundant wildlife, fewer crowds than the Sun Road corridor
  • Hidden Lake Overlook — accessible from Logan Pass visitor center; stunning views, mountain goats

RV Notes

  • ⚠️ Going-to-the-Sun Road vehicle limit is 21 feet total length. Your 18ft RV is likely fine but measure with any hitch/tow setup before driving it through. Oversized vehicles must park at Apgar or St. Mary and take shuttles to Logan Pass.
  • Apgar Campground (west entrance) — great base camp for the west side; reservations on recreation.gov
  • St. Mary Campground (east entrance) — large, open, good for RVs; reservations recommended
  • Many Glacier Campground — beautiful setting but tighter sites; generator use restricted
  • Book campgrounds the day reservations open — they sell out within minutes for July/August

4. 📍 Waterton Lakes National Park, Alberta

From Glacier, head to the northeast side of the park and cross into Canada at the Chief Mountain International Highway border crossing (seasonal — open mid-May to late September). Waterton is literally right across the border — the two parks form the Waterton-Glacier International Peace Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1995.

Border Crossing: Chief Mountain

  • Bring passports for everyone in the vehicle
  • Declare all food — Canada is strict on produce, meats, and plants
  • Turn off propane tanks at the crossing
  • Bear spray: can't cross the border with it — dispose before crossing and buy new cans in Canada
  • Chief Mountain is open roughly mid-May through late September; check Parks Canada for exact dates

Highlights

  • Crypt Lake Hike — arguably the best hike in Canada; requires a boat shuttle, tunnel crawl, and chain-assisted scramble. Book the boat early
  • Red Rock Canyon Parkway — striking red and green quartzite formations
  • Bear's Hump Trail — short steep hike, panoramic views over town and the lake
  • Prince of Wales Hotel Viewpoint — iconic hilltop hotel; great photo
  • Upper Waterton Lake Cruise — crosses back into the US briefly; bring passports
  • Cameron Falls — right in town; beautiful falls with unusual pink quartzite
  • Akamina Parkway — scenic drive to Cameron Lake near the BC border

RV Notes

  • Waterton Townsite Campground is right in town — easy walk to everything; reservations through Parks Canada
  • Town is small and charming; good base for 1–2 nights

5. 📍 Calgary, AB — City Stop (~2.5 hrs north of Waterton)

Calgary is the largest city in Alberta — the last real city stop before heading into the Rockies. After Waterton, drive north on AB-2 to Calgary. This is your best chance to restock everything before the Canada leg of the trip.

Why Stop in Calgary

  • Last major grocery/Costco/Canadian Tire stop before Banff and Jasper
  • Good restaurants and breweries if you want a city night
  • Buy Canadian National Park Pass here if you haven't already (or buy online before leaving home)

Things to Do (if spending a day)

  • Kensington and Inglewood neighborhoods — good food and shops
  • Bow River rafting
  • Calgary Tower views
  • 💡 If visiting in July: Calgary Stampede — largest rodeo in Canada

Canadian National Park Pass

You need this to enter Banff and Jasper. Buy online at reservation.pc.gc.ca. Note: Admission is FREE June 19 – September 7, 2026 — major money saver if your trip overlaps those dates.

6. 📍 Banff National Park, Alberta (~1.5 hrs west of Calgary)

Canada's first national park. The turquoise glacial lakes here are unlike anything else in North America — they have to be seen to be believed.

Highlights

  • Lake Louise — iconic turquoise lake backed by a glacier; hire a canoe or hike to the Lake Agnes Tea House (3.4 mi round-trip)
  • Lake Moraine — even more stunning than Louise; the valley of ten peaks backdrop is unforgettable. ⚠️ Vehicle reservation required in peak season — book through Parks Canada
  • Lake Peyto — wolf-shaped lake visible from Bow Summit viewpoint; brilliant blue
  • Johnston Canyon — walkway through a limestone canyon to lower and upper falls
  • Banff Town — great restaurants, craft breweries, hot springs at Banff Upper Hot Springs
  • Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise — even if not staying, worth a visit

RV Notes

  • Tunnel Mountain Village campgrounds (I and II) in Banff town — full hookups available; convenient location
  • Two Jack Lakeside — beautiful setting, electric hookups; reservations through Parks Canada
  • Lake Moraine road closes to private vehicles in peak season — parking reservation or shuttle required

7. 📍 Yoho National Park, BC (~1 hr west of Banff)

Most people skip Yoho to focus on Banff and Jasper — don't be that person. It's a small park but has some of the most beautiful scenery on the trip. Emerald Lake alone is worth the detour.

Highlights

  • Emerald Lake — vivid green-blue water surrounded by peaks; walk the full 3.3-mile loop
  • Takkakaw Falls — one of Canada's highest waterfalls (1,260 ft); stunning
  • Natural Bridge — river flowing through a carved rock arch; quick stop on the way to Emerald Lake
  • Iceline Trail — one of the best hikes in the Rockies; high alpine meadows and glacier views
  • Kicking Horse River — turquoise glacial melt; whitewater rafting available

RV Notes

  • Kicking Horse Campground — main campground; reservations through Parks Canada
  • ⚠️ Road to Takkakaw Falls has tight switchbacks — assess before driving your 18ft rig. A tow vehicle pulling a trailer must unhook; the RV itself may be fine but go slowly and check current conditions

8. 📍 Icefields Parkway (Highway 93)

National Geographic called this the "drive of a lifetime." 144 miles of Highway 93 connecting Banff to Jasper — glaciers, turquoise lakes, waterfalls, wildlife, and mountains in every direction. Budget a full day; you'll stop constantly. Technically it starts at the Lake Louise junction.

Must-Stop Points (south to north)

  • Herbert Lake — calm mountain reflections
  • Bow Lake — glacier-fed, incredible color; Num-Ti-Jah Lodge sits right on the shore
  • Peyto Lake Viewpoint (Bow Summit) — wolf-shaped lake from above; best view on the parkway
  • Waterfowl Lakes — peaceful double lakes, often uncrowded
  • Mistaya Canyon — short 0.6-mile hike to a dramatic narrow limestone canyon
  • Columbia Icefield — the largest icefield in the Rockies; Athabasca Glacier is walkable from the road. Take the Ice Explorer tour if budget allows
  • Parker Ridge — 3-mile hike to panoramic Saskatchewan Glacier views; one of the best in the park
  • Sunwapta Falls — powerful two-tier waterfall; short walk from parking
  • Athabasca Falls — incredibly powerful despite being short; must stop

RV Notes

  • Pull-outs are plentiful but small — early morning is best for open spots
  • Columbia Icefield has designated RV parking in the large Discovery Centre lot
  • Gas up in Lake Louise (south) and Jasper (north) — no fuel on the parkway itself
  • Wapiti Campground (just south of Jasper) is a great overnight near the north end of the parkway

9. 📍 Jasper National Park, Alberta

The largest national park in the Canadian Rockies. Best wildlife spotting of the whole trip — bears, elk, moose, and bighorn sheep are commonly seen right from the road. Also the world's second largest dark sky preserve. Stargazing here is exceptional.

Highlights

  • Jasper Skytram — Canada's highest and longest aerial tram; views of six mountain ranges from the top
  • Maligne Canyon — deep limestone gorge; good in summer, legendary in winter
  • Maligne Lake — one of the largest glacial lakes in North America; Spirit Island cruise is iconic
  • Spirit Island Boat Cruise — one of the most photographed spots in Canada; book in advance
  • Medicine Lake — mysteriously disappears seasonally due to underground drainage
  • Valley of the Five Lakes — easy 2.9-mile hike to five brilliantly colored lakes
  • Edith Cavell Meadows — hike through wildflower meadows with glacier views; stunning in late July
  • Athabasca River Whitewater Rafting — Class III–IV rapids; guided trips available in town
  • Jasper Planetarium — take advantage of that dark sky designation

RV Notes

  • Whistlers Campground — largest campground in the Canadian Rockies, right outside Jasper; full hookups available
  • Wapiti Campground — open year-round, electrical hookups, great location on the Athabasca River
  • Book Jasper campgrounds early — July/August is extremely busy

10. 🚗 Leg 2: Jasper → Yellowstone via Montana (~11 hrs total driving, 2–3 days)

From Jasper, head back south through Alberta toward the US border. This is a long transit leg — break it up over 2–3 days with overnight stops in Montana.

Route: Jasper → US Border → Yellowstone

Jasper → AB-93 south → Calgary (~230 mi, 4 hrs) → AB-2 south to Lethbridge → Coutts/Sweetgrass border crossing → I-15 south → Great Falls → US-89 south → White Sulphur Springs → Livingston → US-89 south → Gardiner → Yellowstone North Entrance

Border Crossing: Coutts (AB) / Sweetgrass (MT)

  • Open 24/7, year-round — this is the easy crossing
  • Again, declare all food; turn off propane
  • Rebuy bear spray on the US side after crossing

Montana Stops Worth Making

  • Great Falls — Lewis & Clark National Historic Trail Interpretive Center; fueling stop
  • White Sulphur Springs — tiny town with actual hot springs; The Spa Hot Springs Motel has public soaking pools
  • Bozeman — fastest-growing city in Montana; excellent food scene; Museum of the Rockies (world-class dinosaur collection)
  • Livingston — classic Western town just north of Yellowstone; fly-fishing, great bars, good food
  • Gardiner, MT — gateway town at Yellowstone's North Entrance; good last-night stop before entering the park

West Entrance Alternative

If you'd rather enter Yellowstone at the West Entrance (West Yellowstone, MT), cut west from Great Falls toward Missoula and down US-93 to I-90, then US-191 south. Along the way, detour to Virginia City and Nevada City — two well-preserved Wild West ghost towns that are genuinely fascinating.

11. 📍 Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming

The world's first national park. The most geologically unique place on Earth. Give this at least 3–4 days — more if you can. You cannot rush Yellowstone.

Upper Loop (from North Entrance)

  • Mammoth Hot Springs — terraced limestone formations that look like another planet; right near the North Entrance
  • Lamar Valley — the best wildlife watching in the lower 48. Wolves, bison herds, grizzlies, pronghorn. Go at dawn or dusk with binoculars
  • Tower Fall — 132-foot waterfall; easy walk from parking
  • Calcite Springs Overlook — dramatic canyon views and thermal features

Lower Loop

  • Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone — Upper Falls (109 ft) and Lower Falls (308 ft!) are stunning. Visit both the north and south rims. Artist Point is the classic shot
  • Old Faithful — erupts roughly every 90 minutes; the Old Faithful Inn is worth a walk-through
  • Grand Prismatic Spring — largest hot spring in the US; rainbow-colored rings visible from the overlook trail above
  • West Thumb Geyser Basin — geysers and hot pools right on the shore of Yellowstone Lake
  • Hayden Valley — second-best wildlife watching after Lamar; massive bison herds regularly block the road
  • Mud Volcano — bubbling, hissing, sulfurous pools; eerie and fascinating
  • Norris Geyser Basin — the hottest and most active hydrothermal area in the park; Steamboat Geyser (world's tallest active geyser) is here

RV Notes

  • Yellowstone has 12 campgrounds — 5 accept reservations (book 6+ months ahead), rest are first-come
  • Best RV sites: Madison (central, no hookups but great location), Bridge Bay (on Yellowstone Lake), Fishing Bridge RV Park (full hookups — hard-sided RVs only due to bear activity)
  • Generator hours restricted; quiet hours strictly enforced
  • Bison are everywhere and cause frequent road delays — allow extra travel time between sites
  • Bear canisters or bear boxes required for food storage; never leave food in your rig unsecured when away
  • Cell service is extremely limited — download offline maps before you enter

12. 📍 Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming

Drive south from Yellowstone through the John D. Rockefeller Jr. Memorial Parkway and you'll enter Grand Teton without leaving pavement. The Tetons are breathtaking — sheer, jagged peaks rising straight from the valley floor with no foothills to soften the view. This is also the last park stop before heading home to Star.

Highlights

  • Jenny Lake — take the boat across ($18 round-trip) to Inspiration Point and walk down to Hidden Falls; or hike the full 7.2-mile loop around the lake
  • Oxbow Bend — classic Teton mirror reflection shot; excellent wildlife (moose, osprey, bald eagles). Morning is best
  • Schwabacher Landing — another stunning reflection spot; go at sunrise for magic
  • Snake River Overlook — made famous by Ansel Adams' iconic photograph; sweeping valley view
  • Mormon Row — historic homestead barns with the Teton peaks as backdrop; one of the most-photographed spots in Wyoming
  • Signal Mountain Summit Road — drive to the top for a 360° view of the valley and peaks
  • Jackson Lake Lodge — great place for a meal with full Teton views from the window

RV Notes

  • Gros Ventre Campground — largest in the park; great for RVs; first-come or reservation
  • Signal Mountain Campground — on Jackson Lake; reservations required; book early
  • Colter Bay RV Park — full hookups available; the most "developed" option in the park
  • Jackson, WY is 12 miles south of the park — good for final resupply, a restaurant dinner, or one last night before home

13. 🚗 Leg 3: Grand Teton → Star, ID (Home — ~3.5 hrs)

The final drive home from Jackson, WY to Star, ID is a beautiful one — no highway slog. You're following the Snake River valley the whole way.

Route

Grand Teton (Jackson, WY) → US-26/89 west → Alpine, WY → ID-31 west → Victor → Driggs (Teton Valley) → ID-33 west → Rexburg OR ID-31 → Swan Valley → ID-26 west → US-20 → Mountain Home → I-84 west → Star

  • ~230 miles, 3.5 hours without stops
  • Alpine Junction — Wyoming's Palisades Reservoir is gorgeous here; worth a short stop
  • Teton Valley (Victor/Driggs) — the quiet Idaho side of the Tetons; stunning views of the west face of the range that most people never see
  • Swan Valley — beautiful stretch of the Snake River; rainbow and brown trout fishing

Alternative Scenic Route (longer — adds ~45 min)

From Driggs, head north on ID-33 to Ashton, then west on US-20 through Idaho Falls and Twin Falls to I-84. More miles but goes through Idaho Falls for food and fuel.

14. 🚐 18ft RV Tips for This Route

Border Crossings

  • Chief Mountain (going into Canada): Seasonal — open roughly mid-May through late September. Bring passports; declare all food; turn off propane
  • Coutts/Sweetgrass (returning to US): Open 24/7 year-round. Easier and faster crossing. Declare food on the US side too
  • Bear spray: Cannot cross either border with it — dispose before crossing; buy new on the other side

Road Considerations

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road: 21ft total vehicle length limit — measure your rig with hitch before attempting. If over the limit, park at Apgar and take the park shuttle to Logan Pass
  • Takkakaw Falls road (Yoho): Steep switchbacks — check current conditions before committing. May be fine in the 18ft rig without a tow
  • Most campgrounds on this loop handle 18ft rigs well — it's one of the better RV sizes for this trip
  • Keep fuel above half-tank at all times; remote stretches go 80–100+ miles between stations

Booking Strategy

  • Canada: Parks Canada at reservation.pc.gc.ca
  • USA: recreation.gov
  • Yellowstone and Glacier campgrounds sell out within minutes when they open — set a calendar alert and book the moment they drop (typically October–January for following summer)
  • Always have a first-come backup plan for 2–3 nights

Weather & Timing

  • June: Cooler, fewer crowds, some high-elevation roads still closed early in the month; best wildflowers
  • July: Peak season — warm, busy everywhere, everything open
  • August: Watch for wildfire smoke across the region; check AQI before big outdoor days
  • Afternoon thunderstorms are common in the Rockies — plan hikes for mornings
  • Chief Mountain border crossing closes in late September — plan accordingly

15. 📋 Trip Planning Checklist

  • ☐ Valid passports for both US→Canada and Canada→US crossings
  • ☐ US National Park Pass ($80/year — covers Glacier, Yellowstone, Grand Teton)
  • ☐ Canadian National Park Pass (or confirm free dates: June 19–Sep 7, 2026)
  • ☐ Going-to-the-Sun Road vehicle reservation (recreation.gov) — required June 15–Labor Day
  • ☐ Campground reservations: Glacier, Waterton, Banff, Yoho, Jasper, Yellowstone, Grand Teton (Parks Canada + recreation.gov)
  • ☐ Lake Moraine vehicle reservation (peak season — Parks Canada)
  • ☐ Crypt Lake boat shuttle (Waterton)
  • ☐ Spirit Island boat cruise (Maligne Lake, Jasper)
  • ☐ Check Chief Mountain border crossing open dates for your travel window
  • ☐ RV total length measured for Going-to-the-Sun Road compatibility
  • ☐ Offline maps downloaded: Gaia GPS or Google Maps offline for all areas
  • ☐ Bear spray: buy in Montana before Glacier; dispose before Canadian crossing; rebuy in Alberta; dispose before US crossing; rebuy in Montana for Yellowstone/Tetons
  • ☐ Fuel stops mapped for long remote stretches (especially Icefields Parkway, eastern Montana)
  • ☐ AQI and fire restriction check (airnow.gov + Parks Canada) for August dates
