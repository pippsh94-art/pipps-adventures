1. Overview

This road trip is consistently ranked among the best in the world — and for good reason. It links the Canadian Rockies with the iconic US national parks of Glacier, Yellowstone, and Grand Teton. The route runs roughly 1,500–1,800 miles depending on detours. You'll cross the US–Canada border twice.

At a Glance

  • Start: Calgary, Alberta, Canada
  • End: Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming
  • Countries: Canada + USA
  • Key Parks: Banff, Yoho, Jasper, Waterton Lakes (Canada) + Glacier, Yellowstone, Grand Teton (USA)
  • Best Season: June–September (roads fully open, weather cooperative)
  • Minimum Time: 3 weeks (intense pace) — 4 weeks is more comfortable
  • Distance from Star, ID: Glacier NP is ~5.5 hrs; Yellowstone is ~4 hrs — you could do legs of this as separate trips

Route Order

Calgary → Banff → Yoho → Icefields Parkway → Jasper → (back through Calgary) → Waterton Lakes → Glacier NP → south through Montana → Yellowstone → Grand Teton

2. 📍 Calgary, AB — Starting Point

Calgary is the largest city in Alberta and the easiest place to fly into. It's also the last "real city" you'll see for most of the trip — stock up on supplies here.

Things to Do in Calgary

  • Roam the Kensington and Inglewood neighborhoods
  • Raft the Bow River
  • Calgary Tower for city views
  • Walk the RiverWalk
  • Visit Studio Bell (National Music Centre)

💡 If visiting in July: Don't miss the Calgary Stampede — the largest rodeo in Canada.

From Calgary, Banff is about 90 minutes west on the Trans-Canada Highway.

3. 📍 Banff National Park, Alberta

Canada's first national park and still one of its most spectacular. The turquoise glacial lakes here are unlike anything in the lower 48.

Highlights

  • Lake Louise — iconic turquoise lake; hire a canoe or hike up to the Lake Agnes Tea House
  • Lake Moraine — even more stunning than Louise; often on the cover of Canadian travel guides
  • Lake Peyto — brilliant blue from the Bow Summit viewpoint
  • Johnston Canyon — walkway through a limestone canyon to waterfalls
  • Banff Town — great restaurants, breweries, hot springs
  • Stay at the Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise if budget allows

Park Pass

You need a Canadian National Park Pass to enter Banff. Buy online ahead of time to skip the gate line. Note: Admission is free June 19 – September 7, 2026 — score if you're going this summer!

RV Notes

  • Lake Moraine road has a vehicle reservation system in peak season — check Parks Canada website
  • Tunnel Mountain RV Resort in Banff town is popular and has hookups
  • Two Jack Lakeside Campground is beautiful but first-come or reservation required

4. 📍 Yoho National Park, BC

Only 1 hour from Banff and massively underrated. The author of the source article called Emerald Lake her favorite of the entire trip (and she spotted a moose). Don't skip this one.

Highlights

  • Emerald Lake — genuinely stunning; walk the full loop
  • Takkakaw Falls — one of Canada's highest waterfalls
  • Natural Bridge — river flowing through a rock arch
  • Iceline Trail — one of the best hikes in the Rockies
  • Kicking Horse River — brilliant turquoise color

RV Notes

  • Kicking Horse Campground is the main campground — reservations through Parks Canada
  • Road to Takkakaw Falls is steep and winding — check if your 18ft rig can handle it before committing

5. 📍 Icefields Parkway (Highway 93)

National Geographic called this the "drive of a lifetime." 144 miles connecting Banff to Jasper through some of the most breathtaking scenery on Earth. Budget a full day minimum — you'll stop constantly.

Must-Stop Points

  • Herbert Lake — calm reflections of surrounding peaks
  • Bow Lake — glacier-fed, unreal color
  • Peyto Lake Viewpoint — wolf-shaped lake from above
  • Waterfowl Lakes — peaceful and often uncrowded
  • Mistaya Canyon — short hike to a dramatic limestone canyon
  • Columbia Icefield — largest icefield in the Rockies; take the Ice Explorer tour
  • Athabasca Falls — powerful short waterfall, easy walk
  • Sunwapta Falls — two-tier waterfall; gorgeous
  • Parker Ridge — hike for panoramic glacier views

RV Notes

  • Pull-outs are plentiful but can be crowded in peak season — pull through early morning
  • Columbia Icefield parking lot handles RVs; designated areas available
  • Gas up in Banff and Jasper — limited fuel options along the parkway

6. 📍 Jasper National Park, Alberta

The largest national park in the Canadian Rockies. Best place on the trip for wildlife spotting (bears, elk, moose, bighorn sheep are common). Also the world's second largest dark sky preserve — stargazing here is insane.

Highlights

  • Jasper Skytram — Canada's highest and longest aerial tram; stunning views
  • Maligne Canyon — deep limestone gorge, best in winter (ice walks) but great in summer too
  • Maligne Lake — one of the largest glacial lakes in North America
  • Spirit Island — boat cruise to one of the most photographed spots in Canada
  • Medicine Lake — mysterious lake that disappears seasonally due to underground drainage
  • Valley of the Five Lakes — easy hike, five colorful lakes
  • Edith Cavell Meadows — glacier views and wildflowers
  • Athabasca River Rafting — whitewater adventure
  • Jasper Planetarium — great use of that dark sky

RV Notes

  • Whistlers Campground near Jasper has full hookups — largest campground in the Canadian Rockies
  • Wapiti Campground is open year-round and has electrical hookups
  • Book early — Jasper fills up fast in July/August

7. 📍 Waterton Lakes National Park, Alberta

After Jasper, head back east toward Calgary and then south toward the US border. Waterton is the Canadian half of the Waterton-Glacier International Peace Park — a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1995. Smaller and less crowded than the others; don't sleep on it.

Highlights

  • Red Rock Canyon Parkway — striking red and green rock formations
  • Crypt Lake Hike — one of the best hikes in Canada; involves a boat, tunnel, and chain-assisted scrambling
  • Bear's Hump Trail — short steep hike, panoramic town views
  • Prince of Wales Hotel Viewpoint — iconic hilltop hotel photo op
  • Upper Waterton Lake Cruise — crosses into the US (bring passports)
  • Cameron Falls — right in town; beautiful falls with unusual pink quartzite

RV Notes

  • Waterton Townsite Campground is right in town — easy walk to everything
  • Crypt Lake trailhead requires a boat shuttle — book ahead in summer

8. 📍 Glacier National Park, Montana

From Waterton, cross into the US at the Chief Mountain or Carway border crossing. You're now in Montana. Glacier is often overshadowed by Yellowstone but it's every bit as spectacular — possibly more dramatic scenery.

Highlights

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road — 50 miles through the park; one of the most scenic drives in the US. ⚠️ Requires a vehicle reservation in summer (book well ahead)
  • Lake McDonald — crystal clear lake with colorful pebbles; the water is almost otherworldly
  • Lake McDonald Lodge — historic lodge; take a ranger cruise on the lake
  • Avalanche Lake Trail — 4.5 mile round-trip through old-growth cedar to a glacial lake
  • Trail of the Cedars — easy boardwalk through ancient cedar and hemlock
  • Grinnell Glacier Trail — one of the best hikes in the park; see an actual glacier
  • Many Glacier area — wildflower meadows, wildlife, fewer crowds than the Sun Road corridor

RV Notes

  • ⚠️ Going-to-the-Sun Road has a vehicle length limit of 21 feet total length including tow — your 18ft RV may be right at the edge depending on configuration; confirm before driving it through
  • Apgar Campground (west entrance) and St. Mary Campground (east entrance) are the main RV options
  • Many Glacier Campground is beautiful but has tight sites; generator use restricted
  • Reservations open early in the year on recreation.gov — book immediately when they drop

9. 📍 Drive: Glacier → Yellowstone (~6 hours)

This is a long drive. Break it up with stops — there's plenty to see through Montana.

Recommended Stops

  • Columbia Falls — good rodeo scene
  • Whitefish — charming ski/lake town worth an overnight
  • Kalispell — biggest city between Glacier and Missoula
  • Flathead Lake — largest natural freshwater lake west of the Mississippi; cherries in season
  • Missoula — great college town; good food and breweries
  • Deer Lodge — Old Montana Prison and Auto Museum
  • Butte — gritty historic mining city; the Berkeley Pit is wild
  • Bozeman — fastest-growing city in Montana; great food scene; Museum of the Rockies (dinosaurs!)
  • Livingston — fly-fishing hub and cool western town just north of Yellowstone

💡 West Entrance alternative: Virginia City and Nevada City are beautiful Wild West ghost towns — worth the detour if taking the West Entrance to Yellowstone.

Which Entrance?

  • North Entrance (Gardiner, MT): Drive through Bozeman/Livingston; open year-round; enters near Mammoth Hot Springs
  • West Entrance (West Yellowstone, MT): More direct from Missoula; can get busy; enters near Old Faithful corridor
  • Northeast Entrance: Remote, least crowded; enters near Lamar Valley (best wildlife)

10. 📍 Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming

The world's first national park and still the most geologically unique place on Earth. You need multiple days here — don't try to rush it.

Upper Loop Highlights

  • Mammoth Hot Springs — terraced limestone formations that look otherworldly
  • Lamar Valley — the best wildlife watching in the US: wolves, bison, bears, elk. Go at dawn or dusk
  • Tower Fall — 132-foot waterfall
  • Calcite Springs Overlook — dramatic canyon and thermal features

Lower Loop Highlights

  • Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone — Upper Falls, Lower Falls (308 ft!), and Artist Point. Do both the north and south rims
  • Old Faithful — erupts roughly every 90 minutes; predictable and always impressive
  • Grand Prismatic Spring — the largest hot spring in the US; rainbow colors visible from the overlook trail
  • West Thumb Geyser Basin — geysers and hot springs right on Yellowstone Lake
  • Hayden Valley — second best wildlife watching after Lamar; bison jams are common
  • Mud Volcano — bubbling, hissing, sulfur-rich; eerie and cool
  • Norris Geyser Basin — most active and hottest hydrothermal area in the park

RV Notes

  • Yellowstone has 12 campgrounds — 5 accept reservations (book months ahead), the rest are first-come
  • Best RV campgrounds: Madison, Bridge Bay, Fishing Bridge (full hookups)
  • Generator hours are restricted; quiet hours enforced
  • Bison are everywhere — never approach. They can flip a vehicle
  • Bear canisters/bear boxes required for food storage
  • Cell service is limited or nonexistent in most of the park — download offline maps

11. 📍 Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming

Drive south from Yellowstone through the John D. Rockefeller Jr. Memorial Parkway — you'll enter Grand Teton without leaving pavement. The Tetons are jaw-dropping: sharp, jagged peaks rising straight from the valley floor with no foothills to break the view.

Highlights

  • Jenny Lake — take the boat across to Inspiration Point and walk down to Hidden Falls, or hike the full 7-mile loop
  • Oxbow Bend — classic Teton reflection shot; great wildlife (moose, osprey, eagles)
  • Schwabacher Landing — another stunning reflection spot at sunrise
  • Snake River Overlook — made famous by Ansel Adams; sweeping valley view
  • Mormon Row — historic homestead barns with the Tetons as backdrop; iconic photo location
  • Signal Mountain Summit — drive to the top for a panoramic 360° view

RV Notes

  • Gros Ventre Campground — large, beautiful, best bet for RVs; first-come or reservation
  • Signal Mountain Campground — right on Jackson Lake; reservations required
  • Colter Bay RV Park — full hookups available; most "developed" option in the park
  • Jackson, WY is just south — good for resupply, food, and overnight before or after

12. 🚐 18ft RV Tips for This Route

Border Crossing (US ↔ Canada)

  • Bring passports for everyone
  • Declare all food items — Canada is strict; US border is too on meats, fruits, and produce
  • Know your propane rules: propane tanks must be turned off at the border
  • Main crossings: Calgary area (Carway/Coutts) or Chief Mountain (seasonal, near Waterton)

Road Considerations

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road: 21ft total vehicle limit — measure your rig with any tow or hitch before attempting
  • Takkakaw Falls road in Yoho: Steep switchbacks — assess before driving
  • Most campgrounds on this route are fine for 18ft — it's one of the better sizes for this trip
  • Fuel up whenever you see a station; remote stretches can go 100+ miles between towns

Booking Strategy

  • Canada: Reserve through Parks Canada (reservation.pc.gc.ca)
  • USA: Reserve through recreation.gov
  • Book Yellowstone and Glacier campgrounds the moment reservations open — they sell out in minutes for peak summer
  • Have a first-come backup plan for at least 2–3 nights

Weather

  • July is peak season — warm and busy everywhere
  • August: watch for smoke from wildfires in the region; check AQI before days with outdoor plans
  • June: cooler, some high-elevation roads may still be closed early in the month
  • Afternoon thunderstorms are common in the Rockies — plan big hikes for mornings

13. 📋 Trip Planning Checklist

  • ☐ Valid passports for US ↔ Canada border crossings
  • ☐ Canadian National Park Pass (or confirm free dates if going June 19–Sep 7, 2026)
  • ☐ US National Park Pass ($80/year — covers Glacier, Yellowstone, Grand Teton)
  • ☐ Going-to-the-Sun Road vehicle reservation (recreation.gov)
  • ☐ Campground reservations for all nights (Parks Canada + recreation.gov)
  • ☐ Lake Moraine vehicle reservation if going in peak season
  • ☐ Crypt Lake boat shuttle booking (Waterton)
  • ☐ Spirit Island boat cruise booking (Jasper/Maligne Lake)
  • ☐ Offline maps downloaded (Gaia GPS, Maps.me, or Google Maps offline)
  • ☐ Bear spray (can't cross border with it — buy in Canada, dispose before crossing, rebuy in US)
  • ☐ RV length measured for Going-to-the-Sun Road compatibility
  • ☐ Fuel strategy planned (identify gas stops on remote stretches)
  • ☐ AQI and fire restriction check for trip dates
