🌱 Star, Idaho Gardening Guide

Your Growing Zone: 6a (Treasure Valley)

Climate: Hot, dry summers / Cold winters / Big day-night temp swings
Average last frost: May 11-20
Average first frost: October 1-10

🥕 Vegetables That Thrive

Cool-Season Crops (plant early spring or late summer)

  • Potatoes (obviously — Idaho's claim to fame)
  • Lettuce, spinach, kale, chard
  • Peas, broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage
  • Carrots, beets, radishes
  • Onions, garlic

Warm-Season Crops (plant after last frost, May 20+)

  • Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant
  • Beans, squash, zucchini, cucumbers
  • Melons, pumpkins
  • Corn

🌸 Perennials & Flowers

Drought-Tolerant (Perfect for Treasure Valley)

  • Lavender (Lavandula) — thrives in hot, dry summers
  • Russian Sage (Perovskia) — low water, beautiful purple blooms
  • Coneflowers (Echinacea)
  • Black-eyed Susans (Rudbeckia)
  • Yarrow, Sedum

Other Zone 6a Perennials

  • Daylilies, hostas, peonies
  • Salvia, catmint, phlox
  • Ornamental grasses (drought-tolerant)

🌳 Trees & Shrubs

  • Lilacs (Idaho's state flower — love Zone 6a)
  • Apple, pear, cherry trees (great fruit tree zone)
  • Serviceberry, chokecherry (native)
  • Aspen, cottonwood, honeylocust
  • Juniper, arborvitae (evergreens)

💧 Pro Tips for Star Gardening

  1. Water smart — dry climate = drip irrigation or soaker hoses help
  2. Mulch heavily — keeps moisture in, temps stable
  3. Frost dates matter — don't plant tomatoes before May 20, cover tender plants after Oct 1
  4. Amend soil — alkaline soil common, add compost for veggies
  5. Choose drought-tolerant — lavender, Russian sage, native plants = less water, more success

Your zone is great for vegetables (especially cool-season) and drought-tolerant perennials. Perfect for an Idaho garden!
