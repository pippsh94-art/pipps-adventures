💧 Pro Tips for Gardening in Star

1. Water Smart

  • Drip irrigation or soaker hoses are your best friend in this dry climate
  • Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth
  • Mulch heavily (3-4 inches) to retain moisture and regulate soil temperature

2. Amend Your Soil

  • Treasure Valley soil is typically alkaline (pH 7.5-8.5)
  • Add compost, peat moss, or sulfur to lower pH for vegetables
  • Work in organic matter annually to improve drainage and fertility

3. Frost Protection

  • Keep row covers or frost blankets handy in spring and fall
  • Protect tender transplants until after May 20
  • Cover tomatoes, peppers, and squash if frost threatens in late September

4. Choose Drought-Tolerant Plants

  • Native and xeric (low-water) plants will save you time, money, and water
  • Once established, lavender, Russian sage, and sedum need minimal irrigation

5. Plan for Temperature Swings

  • Days can be 85°F+ while nights drop to 50°F in summer
  • Mulch helps buffer these swings and protects roots
  • Heat-loving crops (tomatoes, peppers) appreciate the warm days

6. Extend Your Season

  • Use cold frames, hoop houses, or row covers to start planting earlier and harvest later
  • Plant fall crops (lettuce, spinach, kale) in August for September-October harvest
