Skillet Chili Mac

Delicious blend of heat in this pasta dish. If that's not your thing, swap in green bell peppers for the poblanos!

Prep: 35 minutes • Total: 35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces whole-wheat elbow noodles, uncooked fresh
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 medium poblano peppers and/or green bell peppers
  • 1 medium stalk celery, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 28-ounce can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup low-sodium beef broth
  • 1 15-ounce can low-sodium kidney beans or chili beans, rinsed

Instructions

  1. Cook pasta 2 minutes less than the package directions. Drain.
  2. Meanwhile, heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion, peppers and celery and cook, stirring often, until softened, 4 to 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook, stirring, for 30 seconds. Add beef; cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until the beef is no longer pink, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add tomatoes with their juice, chili powder and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook for 2 minutes. Add broth and the pasta; reduce heat to medium and cook, stirring often, until thick, 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from heat. Fold in sour cream. Serve topped with Cheddar and scallions and/or pickled jalapeños, if desired.
