Spaghetti Squash Casserole

Strands of tender squash replace pasta for a more flavorful version of spaghetti pie. A sprinkling of nutty fontina cheese melts on top beautifully.

Prep: 25 minutes • Cook: 60 minutes • Total: 1 hour 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 2 1/2- to 3-pound spaghetti squash, cut in half lengthwise, seeds removed
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 medium shallot, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt plus 1/8 teaspoon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1 28-ounce can no-salt-added crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup shredded fontina cheese
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Place squash, cut-side down, in a microwave-safe dish; add water. Microwave, uncovered, on high until tender, about 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, use squash halves cut side down on a trimmed baking sheet. Bake at 400°F until tender, 40 to 50 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, cook ground beef in a large saucepan skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up with a wooden spoon, until no longer pink, 5 to 7 minutes. Add shallot, garlic, Italian seasoning, salt and pepper and cook, stirring, for 1 minute. Stir in tomatoes and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to maintain a simmer. Cook until thickened, about 15 minutes. Transfer to shells into the sauce; stir to combine. Transfer the pan to the oven. Continue baking until bubbling, about 15 minutes. Let stand 5 minutes. Serve sprinkled with basil, if desired.
