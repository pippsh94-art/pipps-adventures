Keto Aboriginal Australian Bush Tucker Kangaroo

Traditional Australian outback cuisine with native spices and bush ingredients. Ancient Aboriginal wisdom meets modern keto.

#0174

⏰ Timing

Prep: 30 minutes
Cook: 2 hours
Total: 2 hours 30 minutes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 pounds kangaroo roast (or venison)
  • 2 tablespoons bush tomato (or sun-dried tomatoes), ground
  • 1 tablespoon wattleseed (or sesame seeds), ground
  • 1 teaspoon lemon myrtle (or lemon zest)
  • 1 teaspoon native pepperberry (or black pepper)
  • 2 tablespoons macadamia oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups native broth (or beef stock)
  • 1/4 cup native finger limes (or regular lime juice)
  • Bush honey substitute (erythritol)
  • Salt to taste
  • Native greens for serving

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Season kangaroo roast with salt and native pepperberry.
  2. Mix ground bush tomato, wattleseed, and lemon myrtle to make a spice rub.
  3. Rub spice mixture all over the meat and let sit for 20 minutes.
  4. Preheat oven to 325°F for slow roasting.
  5. Heat macadamia oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  6. Sear kangaroo roast on all sides until well-browned, about 8 minutes total.
  7. Remove meat and add sliced onion to the pot.
  8. Cook onion until softened and golden, about 5 minutes.
  9. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  10. Return roast to pot and add native broth.
  11. Cover and transfer to oven.
  12. Braise for 1.5-2 hours until meat is tender but not dry.
  13. Kangaroo can become tough if overcooked.
  14. Remove roast and let rest for 10 minutes.
  15. Strain cooking liquid and reduce on stovetop until thickened.
  16. Finish sauce with finger lime juice and honey substitute.
  17. Slice roast and serve with native greens and the reduced sauce.
  18. This dish connects us to 50,000 years of Aboriginal culinary wisdom.
