Keto Chinese Peking Duck

Classic Beijing roast duck with crispy skin and pancakes. Traditional technique adapted for keto with all authentic flavors.

#0153

⏰ Timing

Prep: 45 minutes
Cook: 3 hours
Total: 2 days (including air-drying)

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 whole duck (4-5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons five-spice powder
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey substitute
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 green onions
  • 4 slices ginger
  • Keto pancakes (almond flour based)
  • Hoisin sauce (sugar-free)
  • Cucumber julienne
  • Green onion brushes

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Clean duck inside and out, pat dry completely.
  2. Stuff cavity with green onions and ginger slices.
  3. Truss duck and prick skin all over with a fork (don't pierce meat).
  4. Blanch duck in boiling water for 2 minutes to tighten skin.
  5. Mix five-spice, soy sauce, honey substitute, rice wine, and salt.
  6. Rub spice mixture all over duck.
  7. Hang duck in a cool, airy place for 12-24 hours to air-dry skin.
  8. The skin should feel papery and taut.
  9. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  10. Place duck breast-side up on rack in roasting pan.
  11. Roast for 30 minutes, then reduce to 350°F.
  12. Continue roasting for 1.5-2 hours until skin is mahogany colored.
  13. Internal temperature should reach 165°F.
  14. Let rest for 15 minutes before carving.
  15. Serve carved duck with keto pancakes, hoisin sauce, cucumber, and green onions.
  16. Traditional method: wrap duck in pancake with condiments.
