Keto Polynesian Breadfruit Substitute Taro Pork

Traditional Pacific island cooking with taro and pork wrapped in leaves. Ancient island techniques adapted for keto living.

#0176

⏰ Timing

Prep: 40 minutes
Cook: 3 hours
Total: 3 hours 40 minutes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 4 pounds pork shoulder, cubed
  • 2 pounds taro root (or turnips), cubed
  • Large banana leaves (or parchment paper)
  • 1/4 cup sea salt
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 whole coconut, flesh grated
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Hot volcanic rocks (or oven method)
  • Ti leaves for wrapping (or foil)
  • Tropical herbs for garnish

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Traditional method uses an underground oven (imu), but we'll adapt for home cooking.
  2. Preheat oven to 275°F for slow, gentle cooking.
  3. Season pork cubes generously with sea salt.
  4. Let pork sit for 30 minutes to draw out moisture.
  5. Heat coconut oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  6. Brown pork cubes in batches until golden on all sides.
  7. Remove pork and set aside.
  8. In the same pot, cook onion until softened, about 5 minutes.
  9. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  10. Layer banana leaves in the bottom of the pot.
  11. Add pork, taro cubes, and grated coconut in layers.
  12. Sprinkle with fish sauce and any remaining salt.
  13. Cover with more banana leaves, then seal tightly with lid.
  14. Cook in oven for 2.5-3 hours until pork is fork-tender.
  15. The banana leaves will steam everything to perfection.
  16. Carefully remove leaves and check for doneness.
  17. Serve family-style with the aromatic cooking juices.
  18. Garnish with tropical herbs and enjoy this island feast.
  19. This method preserves the authentic flavors of Polynesian cooking.
