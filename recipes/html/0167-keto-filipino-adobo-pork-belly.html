Keto Filipino Adobo Pork Belly

Philippines' national dish with soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic. Sweet, sour, and savory harmony in every tender bite.

#0167

⏰ Timing

Prep: 15 minutes
Cook: 1 hour 30 minutes
Total: 1 hour 45 minutes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 pounds pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup coconut aminos (instead of soy sauce)
  • 1/3 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, cloves smashed
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon erythritol (optional)
  • Green onions for garnish
  • Steamed cauliflower rice for serving

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Cut pork belly into 2-inch chunks, leaving skin on if desired.
  2. In a large bowl, combine coconut aminos, vinegar, smashed garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns.
  3. Add pork chunks and marinate for 30 minutes, tossing occasionally.
  4. Heat coconut oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  5. Remove pork from marinade (reserve marinade) and brown on all sides.
  6. This should take about 8-10 minutes total.
  7. Add sliced onion and cook until softened, about 3 minutes.
  8. Pour in reserved marinade and add water.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  10. Simmer covered for 45 minutes until pork is tender.
  11. Remove lid and simmer for 30 more minutes until sauce reduces.
  12. Add fish sauce and erythritol if using.
  13. The sauce should be thick and glossy.
  14. Remove bay leaves before serving.
  15. Garnish with green onions and serve over cauliflower rice.
