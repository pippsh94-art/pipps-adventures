Keto Miocene Grassland Revolution Grazing Herds

The rise of grasslands and the megafauna that grazed them. Vast herds and their predators in the expanding savannas.

#0212

⏰ Timing

Prep: 23-5 million years ago
Cook: Grassland cooking methods
Total: Miocene epoch

🛒 Ingredients

  • Paraceratherium giant rhinoceros meat
  • Deinotherium massive elephant proteins
  • Amphicyon bear-dog predator essence
  • Hipparion three-toed horse flavors
  • Gomphotheres early elephant variations
  • C4 grassland photosynthesis compounds
  • Savanna ecosystem seasonings
  • Miocene climate cooling spices
  • Megafauna gigantism factors
  • Predator-prey relationship enhancers

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Create dish representing the greatest terrestrial mammals ever.
  2. Use paraceratherium giant rhinoceros meat from the largest land mammal ever.
  3. Add deinotherium massive elephant proteins with downward-curving tusks.
  4. Include amphicyon bear-dog essence representing dominant Miocene predators.
  5. Use hipparion three-toed horse flavors from grassland-adapted equids.
  6. Add gomphotheres early elephant proteins showing elephant diversification.
  7. Season with C4 grassland photosynthesis compounds from new grass types.
  8. Use savanna ecosystem seasonings from expanding grassland habitats.
  9. Include Miocene climate cooling spices from continued global cooling.
  10. Add megafauna gigantism factors that produced the largest land mammals.
  11. Cook using grassland methods - open fire grilling representing savanna conditions.
  12. Everything should be oversized representing Miocene megafauna scale.
  13. The dish tells the story of grassland expansion driving mammalian evolution.
  14. Predator-prey relationships become increasingly sophisticated.
  15. Serve on massive platters representing the scale of Miocene life.
  16. This represents the peak of mammalian size and diversity.
  17. The golden age of grassland megafauna.
