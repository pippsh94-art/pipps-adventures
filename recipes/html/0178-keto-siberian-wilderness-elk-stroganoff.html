Keto Siberian Wilderness Elk Stroganoff

Russian taiga survival cuisine with wild elk and forest mushrooms. Siberian frontier cooking adapted for ketogenic nutrition.

#0178

⏰ Timing

Prep: 25 minutes
Cook: 1 hour 45 minutes
Total: 2 hours 10 minutes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 2.5 pounds elk sirloin (or venison), sliced thin
  • 1 pound mixed wild mushrooms (porcini, chanterelles)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup vodka
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon juniper berries, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill for garnish
  • Sauerkraut for serving

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Slice elk meat against the grain into thin strips.
  2. Season with salt, pepper, and crushed juniper berries.
  3. Clean wild mushrooms and slice thick to maintain texture.
  4. Heat butter and olive oil in a large cast iron pan over high heat.
  5. Sear elk strips in batches for 2-3 minutes until browned but still pink inside.
  6. Remove elk and set aside - don't overcook or it will be tough.
  7. In the same pan, cook sliced onion until golden, about 8 minutes.
  8. Add mushrooms and cook until they release their liquid and caramelize.
  9. Add garlic and caraway seeds, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  10. Deglaze pan with vodka, scraping up any browned bits.
  11. Let alcohol cook off, then add bay leaves.
  12. Reduce heat to low and stir in sour cream and heavy cream.
  13. Whisk in Dijon mustard until smooth.
  14. Return elk to the pan along with any accumulated juices.
  15. Simmer very gently for 3-4 minutes to warm through.
  16. Don't let it boil or the cream will curdle.
  17. Remove bay leaves and taste for seasoning.
  18. Garnish with fresh dill and serve immediately.
  19. Traditional accompaniment is sauerkraut and dark bread.
  20. This hearty dish sustained Siberian hunters through harsh winters.
