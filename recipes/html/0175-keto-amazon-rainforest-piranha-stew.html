Keto Amazon Rainforest Piranha Stew

Indigenous Amazon basin cuisine with tropical river fish and jungle herbs. Rainforest survival meets ketogenic nutrition.

#0175

⏰ Timing

Prep: 35 minutes
Cook: 45 minutes
Total: 1 hour 20 minutes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 pounds whole piranha (or red snapper), cleaned
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil (or coconut oil)
  • 1 large cassava root, cubed (or turnip substitute)
  • 2 plantains, sliced (omit for strict keto)
  • 4 Brazil nuts, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons açaí powder
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 jalapeño peppers, minced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 4 cups fish stock
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Jungle herbs for garnish

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Clean piranha thoroughly, removing scales and fins.
  2. Cut into serving-sized pieces, keeping bones for flavor.
  3. Season fish pieces with salt, pepper, and lime juice.
  4. Let marinate for 20 minutes.
  5. Heat palm oil in a large, heavy pot over medium heat.
  6. Sear fish pieces briefly, about 2 minutes per side.
  7. Remove fish and set aside.
  8. In the same pot, cook onions until golden, about 6 minutes.
  9. Add garlic, jalapeños, and cumin. Cook for 2 minutes.
  10. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down, about 8 minutes.
  11. Add cubed cassava and fish stock.
  12. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  13. Return fish to the pot along with any accumulated juices.
  14. Add chopped Brazil nuts and açaí powder.
  15. Simmer gently for 10-15 minutes until fish is cooked through.
  16. Stir in fresh cilantro just before serving.
  17. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and additional lime juice.
  18. Garnish with jungle herbs and serve in traditional wooden bowls.
  19. This stew represents the incredible biodiversity of the Amazon.
