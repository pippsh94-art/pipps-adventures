Keto Mongolian Fermented Mare's Milk Lamb

Traditional steppe nomad cuisine with fermented dairy and lamb. Ancient Mongolian pastoral traditions made keto-friendly.

#0177

⏰ Timing

Prep: 30 minutes
Cook: 2 hours 30 minutes
Total: 3 hours

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 pounds lamb leg, bone-in
  • 2 cups kefir (substitute for airag)
  • 1/4 cup aged cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons caraway seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cups lamb stock
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • Wild herbs (or thyme and rosemary)
  • Fermented vegetables for serving

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Score the lamb leg in a crosshatch pattern to help flavors penetrate.
  2. Rub lamb with salt, white pepper, and half the caraway seeds.
  3. Marinate lamb in kefir for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. This tenderizes the meat like traditional fermented mare's milk.
  5. Remove lamb from marinade and pat dry (reserve marinade).
  6. Preheat oven to 325°F.
  7. Heat butter in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  8. Brown lamb leg on all sides until golden, about 8 minutes total.
  9. Remove lamb and add sliced onion to the pot.
  10. Cook onion until caramelized, about 10 minutes.
  11. Add garlic, remaining caraway seeds, coriander seeds, and bay leaves.
  12. Cook for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  13. Return lamb to pot and add reserved kefir marinade and lamb stock.
  14. Add vinegar and crumbled cheese.
  15. Bring to a gentle simmer, then cover and transfer to oven.
  16. Braise for 2-2.5 hours until lamb is fall-off-the-bone tender.
  17. Remove bay leaves and let rest for 10 minutes.
  18. Slice lamb and serve with the tangy braising liquid.
  19. Garnish with wild herbs and serve with fermented vegetables.
  20. This dish captures the essence of nomadic Mongolian cuisine.
