Keto Ethiopian Berbere Chicken

Spicy Ethiopian chicken stew with berbere spice blend and hard-boiled eggs. Bold, complex flavors from East Africa.

#0148

⏰ Timing

Prep: 25 minutes
Cook: 45 minutes
Total: 1 hour 10 minutes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3 pounds chicken, cut into pieces
  • 6 hard-boiled eggs
  • 2 large red onions, finely chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons berbere spice blend
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. If you can't find berbere spice, make your own by mixing paprika, cayenne, fenugreek, coriander, cardamom, and other Ethiopian spices.
  2. Season chicken pieces with salt and let sit for 15 minutes.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat.
  4. Add chopped onions and cook for 15-20 minutes until very soft and golden.
  5. Add garlic, ginger, berbere spice, paprika, cardamom, cinnamon, and turmeric.
  6. Cook for 2 minutes until very fragrant.
  7. Add tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes.
  8. Add chicken pieces and brown on all sides, about 8 minutes total.
  9. Pour in chicken broth and bring to a simmer.
  10. Cover and simmer for 25-30 minutes until chicken is tender.
  11. Add hard-boiled eggs and simmer for 5 more minutes.
  12. Stir in lemon juice and adjust seasoning.
  13. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  14. Traditionally served with injera bread, but great with cauliflower rice.
