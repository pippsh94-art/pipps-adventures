Keto Peruvian Ceviche

Fresh fish 'cooked' in citrus juices with onions, peppers, and cilantro. Light, refreshing, and bursting with coastal flavors.

#0149

⏰ Timing

Prep: 30 minutes
Cook: 0 minutes
Total: 2 hours 30 minutes (including marinating)

🛒 Ingredients

  • 2 pounds fresh white fish (halibut or sea bass), cubed
  • 1 cup fresh lime juice (about 12-15 limes)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 aji amarillo peppers (or jalapeños), minced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 sweet potato, boiled and sliced (optional)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • Salt and white pepper to taste
  • Lettuce leaves for serving
  • Corn nuts for garnish (optional)

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Ensure fish is sushi-grade quality and very fresh.
  2. Cut fish into 1/2-inch cubes and place in a non-reactive bowl.
  3. Add lime juice, lemon juice, minced peppers, garlic, and ginger.
  4. The citrus should completely cover the fish.
  5. Season with salt and white pepper.
  6. Cover and refrigerate for 2 hours until fish is 'cooked' by the acid.
  7. The fish will turn opaque white when ready.
  8. Drain most of the citrus marinade, leaving just a little.
  9. Add sliced red onion and let sit for 10 minutes.
  10. Gently fold in chopped cilantro and diced cucumber.
  11. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and more lime if needed.
  12. Serve immediately in chilled bowls over lettuce leaves.
  13. Garnish with avocado slices and sweet potato if using.
  14. Best enjoyed fresh - don't let it sit too long after final preparation.
