Abalone Chowder

Original cookbook page 779

Ingredients

  • 2-3 Abalone (depends on size)
  • 2 Ibs bacon (can use less)
  • 5 potatoes (I used 3 Lg Russet and 2 white potatoes) - diced
  • 2 celery stalks (maybe 3) - chopped
  • 1 lg onion - chopped
  • butter & flour (roux ~ I used % cup each but could use more)
  • cream or half & half
  • white corn
  • bay leaves
  • Old Bay
  • Salt & pepper

Instructions

  1. I cooked the bacon and set between paper towels to eliminate oil Pop ab, slice and POUND until tender Ina large pot, I puta combo of water and chicken broth about 4 way up (or less), enough to cover ab. Bring to a boil and put pounded Ab in to cook it. Add salt & pepper While Ab is cooking, sauté onion and celery until soft When Ab is done, remove the Ab with a slotted spoon and set aside. I chopped big pieces up to make them smaller. Remove some of the water and set aside in case you need it for potatoes etc, In the Ab water, boil the potatoes. Add in the onion/celery mixture toward the end. Add the Ab back into the pot. Add the bay leaves (maybe add earlier!) Make the roux. Add roux and some cream to the pot. If more liquid is needed, add some of the water back. I may make more roux next time. Add the Old Bay and any salt/pepper needed to taste. Add in the corn. Crumble bacon and add as well. Simmer for 20 min.
