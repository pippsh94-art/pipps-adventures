Smoky Breakfast Pizza

Original cookbook page 184

Ingredients

  • 1 (12-in.) prepared pizza dough or store-bought pizza crust
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  • 6 slices hickory smoked bacon
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 to 2 oz. smoked mozzarella cheese
  • Shredded (about to taste)
  • 1 oz. aged cheddar cheese, finely grated (about to taste)
  • 1 cup thinly sliced fresh mixed wild mushrooms (about 4 oz.)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Crushed red pepper, to taste
  • Large eggs

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F with a rack in the lower-third position until smoking, about 25 seconds. Spread oil around the pizza (13- to 14-inch), brushing liberally to coat evenly, especially the top.
  2. Preheat oven to 450°F with racks in upper-third and lower-third positions. Add prepared pizza dough to cast iron skillet or pizza pan. Brush with remaining oil and top with cheese. Broil until bacon is rendered and cooked through, about 2 to 3 minutes. Broil until edges of crust begin to brown, 7 to 8 minutes.
  3. Scatter dough crust lightly with flour (optional), and top with smoked mozzarella, shredded cheddar to taste, and cooked mushrooms. Bake until golden, about 5 to 7 minutes total.
  4. Remove pizza from oven. Using the back of a spoon, create 4 small wells (about 2-in. diameter) in the top. Crack eggs into each well. Return pizza to oven and bake until egg whites are set and yolks are still runny, 5 to 7 minutes. Top with large pieces of bacon and fresh basil. Serve immediately.
