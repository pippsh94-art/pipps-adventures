Lamb Shanks

Original cookbook page 96

Ingredients

  • Mi Braised Lamb Shanks
  • aa)o tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, peeled
  • Stalks, diced

Instructions

  1. Season the lamb shanks. Sprinkle salt and pepper generously over the lamb shanks.
  2. Heat the oil. Pour the oil into a large dutch oven or another oven-safe dish and place it over medium high heat. Allow it to heat completely, until it begins to smoke just slightly.
  3. Sear the lamb shanks. Place the seasoned lamb shanks into the hot oil and sear on all sides until well browned.
  4. Add the vegetables, peppercorns and wine. Arrange the vegetables and garlic cloves around the lamb shanks, and drop in the peppercorns. Pour the wine over the entire contents of the pot. Let the red wine come to a boil, and boil it for three minutes. Add the water and reduce the heat to bring everything to a quick simmer.
  5. Braise the lamb shanks. Cover the dutch oven and place it in a preheated 350°F oven. Cook for 2 to 2.5 hours, until the meat is very tender and falling off the bone.
  6. Strain and reduce the braising liquid. Transfer the cooked lamb shanks to a serving platter. Pour the braising liquid through a fine-mesh sieve to strain out the vegetables and the solids. Pour the liquid into a saucepan and cook it over medium heat, stirring often, until it has reduced and become a thick sauce.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. To make the sauce thicker, add a teaspoon of cornstarch.
