Brussels Sprouts Gratin

Original cookbook page 117

A light cheese sauce made with Gruyère and Parmesan – the perfect Holiday dish! From www.skinnytaste.com. Rich and creamy comfort side dish.

Prep: 15 minutes • Cook: 40 minutes • Total: 55 minutes

Ingredients

  • 16 oz brussels sprouts (trimmed of outer leaves and sliced in half)
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil spray
  • ½ tablespoon butter
  • ⅓ cup chopped shallots
  • 2 teaspoons all purpose flour (or gluten free flour for GF)
  • ¾ cup fat free milk (may need more for desired consistency)
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme
  • 1 tablespoon grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 oz grated Gruyère cheese, divided

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Spray an 8"×12" gratin dish or casserole with olive oil.
  2. Add the brussels sprouts and season with salt and pepper. Spray more olive oil over the brussels and place in the lower third of the oven.
  3. Bake 15 minutes, toss and bake an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, heat a medium nonstick pan over medium heat. Add butter and let it melt, add the shallots and cook until softened, about 4 to 5 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle the flour over the shallots to make a roux, whisking for 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. Add the milk and stir with a wooden spoon until the roux is incorporated into the milk. Cook over medium-low heat for about 4 minutes, stirring, until the sauce thickens.
  7. Add fresh thyme, parmesan and half of the grated Gruyère cheese into the white sauce and stir until the cheese is melted and incorporated into the sauce.
  8. Pour over the brussels sprouts, and top with the remaining cheese.
  9. Bake for 15 minutes until top is lightly browned and bubbly.

📝 Recipe Notes

SAUCE TIP: If using 1% milk, sauce may be very thick. Add more milk and water to make it pourable if needed. Adjust liquid to desired consistency. 110 kcal per ½ cup serving, 12g carbohydrates. Combined recipe from pages 117-118.
