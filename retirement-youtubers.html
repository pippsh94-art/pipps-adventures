1. Heritage Wealth Planning

Creator: Josh Scandlen YouTube: Heritage Wealth Planning

Josh is a great resource for retirement planning. Full disclosure — I paid for his services, and it was well worth it. He has a large library of videos covering a wide range of retirement topics, and it’s worth scanning through for subjects relevant to your situation.

Why I Like Him

  • Decades of experience in financial planning with a practical, no-nonsense approach.
  • Conservative, straightforward style — a God-fearing man from Georgia who connects well with people from all walks of life.
  • The Barbell Approach — his investment strategy is simple, easy to understand, and manageable for self-directed investors.
  • RightCapital — this is his Monte Carlo simulation tool that he sets up and runs for clients. He uses pretty conservative settings, so if your plan works in his model, you’re in great shape.

Books by Josh Scandlen

Josh has written several books on retirement planning, available on Amazon:

  • Books by Josh Scandlen on Amazon

Community

Josh hosts a community discussion on Locals where he provides ongoing insights and interacts with his audience.

2. Holy Schmidt!

Creator: Holy Schmidt YouTube: Holy Schmidt!

This creator delivers great detail on retirement topics with a classic dry accounting demeanor. If you want thorough, numbers-driven analysis, Holy Schmidt is your channel.

Why I Like Him

  • Deeply detailed content — covers topics with the precision you’d expect from someone with an accounting background.
  • Huge video library — videos galore on nearly every retirement topic you can think of.
  • Great second opinion — I use his channel as another viewpoint alongside Josh Scandlen to get a well-rounded perspective on retirement strategies.

3. Joe Kuhn

Creator: Joe Kuhn YouTube: Joe Kuhn

Joe is a retired guy from Alcoa with a classic Midwest dry, sardonic humor. He provides good practical advice and real-world tips from someone who has actually walked the walk.

Why I Like Him

  • Real-world experience — retired from Alcoa, so he’s living what he teaches.
  • Practical advice — down-to-earth tips that you can actually implement.
  • Midwest humor — makes the content enjoyable with his dry wit.
  • Bucket approach — Joe led me into the bucket strategy, which I like as a logical segregation of funds.
  • Preventative maintenance background — his actual career was in preventative maintenance, and he brings that practical, “prevent problems before they happen” mindset to retirement planning.

Tools He Recommends

Joe frequently links to these Monte Carlo simulation tools in his videos:

  • Money Pickle — connects you with financial advisors and planning tools.
  • Boldin (formerly NewRetirement) — a comprehensive retirement planning calculator with Monte Carlo simulations. Great for running your own scenarios.

4. Azul

Creator: Azul Wells YouTube: Azul

A former financial consultant with massive experience. Azul is as smooth as you’d expect from someone with his background.

Why I Like Him

  • Deep industry experience — years as a financial consultant give him genuine inside knowledge.
  • Process-oriented thinking — he’s more of a process guy, which is valuable for understanding the “why” behind financial decisions.
  • Inside perspective — a good source for understanding how the financial industry actually works and thinks.
  • Note: Can be less clear on some specific details, but overall a solid source for big-picture retirement thinking.

5. Duane (Retire Early 500K)

Creator: Duane YouTube: Retire Early 500K

Duane is a straight-shooting Southern California guy — the kind of older, no-nonsense Californian you used to see everywhere in the state. He retired early and wants you to know you can too.

Why I Like Him

  • Brutally straightforward — cuts out the cruft and tells you to stop overthinking it.
  • DIY mentality — his message is clear: you can do this yourself, because he did.
  • No fluff — doesn’t waste your time with elaborate production or filler content. Gets right to the point.
  • Real experience — he actually retired early on 500K and shares what that looks like in practice.

6. Tae Kim (Financial Tortoise)

Creator: Tae Kim YouTube: Financial Tortoise

Tae Kim’s channel was instrumental for me in setting goals and figuring out where to begin my retirement planning journey.

Why I Like Him

  • Great starting point — if you’re just beginning your retirement planning, this is an excellent place to start.
  • Investing order — provides clear guidance on the order in which you should invest (401k, IRA, taxable, etc.).
  • Fund recommendations — has a good list of funds he likes, making it easy to get started without analysis paralysis.
  • Solid reference material — the kind of channel you bookmark and come back to when you need to double-check your approach.

7. Erin Talks Money

Creator: Erin YouTube: Erin Talks Money

Erin brings a younger perspective to retirement planning and the bucket strategy. She’s sharp, engaging, and genuinely fun to listen to.

Why I Like Her

  • Bucket strategy deep dives — another great take on the bucket approach to retirement planning, complementing Joe Kuhn’s perspective.
  • Cost-conscious content — she offers lots of tips on fund costs and expense ratios, which is what originally drew me in.
  • Younger viewpoint — brings a fresh generational perspective to retirement planning that the older creators sometimes miss.
  • Entertaining delivery — smart and easy to listen to, and her blooper reel is worth a few chuckles.

How I Use These Channels

I don’t rely on just one source. Each creator brings a different perspective:

  • Josh Scandlen for the big-picture strategy and professional guidance
  • Holy Schmidt for deep-dive analysis and a second opinion on the numbers
  • Joe Kuhn for practical, real-world “boots on the ground” retirement wisdom
  • Azul for insider financial industry perspective and process thinking
  • Duane for the no-BS reality check — can you actually do this yourself? Yes.
  • Tae Kim for getting started and building a solid investment foundation
  • Erin Talks Money for cost-conscious planning and a fresh perspective

Cross-referencing multiple viewpoints helps you build confidence in your plan. No single YouTube channel has all the answers, but together they give you a solid foundation.
