1. Heritage Wealth Planning

Creator: Josh Scandlen YouTube: Heritage Wealth Planning

Josh is a great resource for retirement planning. Full disclosure — I paid for his services, and it was well worth it. He has a large library of videos covering a wide range of retirement topics, and it’s worth scanning through for subjects relevant to your situation.

Why I Like Him

  • Decades of experience in financial planning with a practical, no-nonsense approach.
  • Conservative, straightforward style — a God-fearing man from Georgia who connects well with people from all walks of life.
  • The Barbell Approach — his investment strategy is simple, easy to understand, and manageable for self-directed investors.
  • RightCapital — this is his Monte Carlo simulation tool that he sets up and runs for clients. He uses pretty conservative settings, so if your plan works in his model, you’re in great shape.

Community

2. Holy Schmidt!

Creator: Holy Schmidt YouTube: Holy Schmidt!

This creator delivers great detail on retirement topics with a classic dry accounting demeanor. If you want thorough, numbers-driven analysis, Holy Schmidt is your channel.

Why I Like Him

  • Deeply detailed content — covers topics with the precision you’d expect from someone with an accounting background.
  • Huge video library — there’s content on nearly every retirement topic you can think of.
  • Great second opinion — I use his channel as another viewpoint alongside Josh Scandlen to get a well-rounded perspective on retirement strategies.

3. Joe Kuhn

Creator: Joe Kuhn YouTube: Joe Kuhn

Joe is a retired guy from Alcoa with a classic Midwest dry, sardonic humor. He provides good practical advice and real-world tips from someone who has actually walked the walk.

Why I Like Him

  • Real-world experience — retired from Alcoa, so he’s living what he teaches.
  • Practical advice — down-to-earth tips that you can actually implement.
  • Midwest humor — makes the content enjoyable with his dry wit.
  • Preventative maintenance background — his actual career was in preventative maintenance, and he brings that practical, “prevent problems before they happen” mindset to retirement planning.

Tools He Recommends

Joe frequently links to these Monte Carlo simulation tools in his videos:

  • Money Pickle — connects you with financial advisors and planning tools.
  • Boldin (formerly NewRetirement) — a comprehensive retirement planning calculator with Monte Carlo simulations. Great for running your own scenarios.

How I Use These Channels

I don’t rely on just one source. Each creator brings a different perspective:

  • Josh Scandlen for the big-picture strategy and professional guidance
  • Holy Schmidt for deep-dive analysis and a second opinion on the numbers
  • Joe Kuhn for practical, real-world “boots on the ground” retirement wisdom

Cross-referencing multiple viewpoints helps you build confidence in your plan. No single YouTube channel has all the answers, but together they give you a solid foundation.
