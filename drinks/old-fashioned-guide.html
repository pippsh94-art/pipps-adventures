The Old Fashioned: A Timeless Classic 🥃

Your guide to building a perfect Old Fashioned, from classic to creative twists

How to Build a Better Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned is deceptively simple: spirit, sugar, bitters, water. But like any true classic, the devil is in the details – quality ingredients, proper ratios, and a little technique make all the difference.

The Anatomy of an Old Fashioned

  • The Spirit: Traditionally Bourbon or Rye whiskey. Bourbon is sweeter and softer, Rye is spicier and drier. Pick one you enjoy sipping neat.
  • The Sweetener: Simple syrup is consistent and easy. Sugar cubes often need a touch more water to dissolve. Maple syrup or Demerara syrup can be fun variations.
  • The Bitters: Angostura is the classic. Orange bitters are a common and excellent addition. Experiment with others!
  • The Dilution: Crucial. A large, dense ice cube (or sphere) melts slowly, chilling the drink without over-diluting it too quickly. Stirring is key for proper integration and chilling.

The Classic Old Fashioned (Bourbon or Rye)

This is the bedrock. Master this, and you can build anything.

Ingredients & Ratios

Ingredient Amount Notes
Whiskey (Bourbon or Rye) 2 oz Your choice: Bourbon for a sweeter profile, Rye for a spicier kick.
Simple Syrup (1:1 ratio) ¼ oz Make it yourself: equal parts sugar and hot water, stir until dissolved. Let cool.
Angostura Bitters 2-3 dashes The classic. Don’t skip these!
Orange Peel 1 strip For garnish and aromatic oils.
Large Ice Cube 1 large Or a sphere. Melts slower, less dilution.

Instructions

  1. Combine: In an Old Fashioned glass (rocks glass), add the simple syrup and bitters.
  2. Stir: Add the whiskey. Stir briefly to combine the liquids.
  3. Ice: Add your large ice cube.
  4. Stir Again: Stir slowly for 30-60 seconds. This is critical for chilling and proper dilution. You want the drink to be cold, but not watery. Taste as you go.
  5. Garnish: Express the oils from an orange peel over the drink by twisting it over the glass, then drop it in (or drape over the rim).

Old Fashioned Variations: Twist it Up!

Once you’ve mastered the classic, these variations open up a world of flavor.

1. The Smoked Old Fashioned (Bourbon or Rye) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Adds a beautiful aroma and depth of flavor.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Whiskey (Bourbon or Rye) 2 oz A bolder Bourbon or higher-proof Rye stands up well to smoke.
Demerara Syrup (2:1 ratio) ¼ oz 2 parts Demerara sugar to 1 part hot water. Adds more richness.
Angostura Bitters 2 dashes Classic.
Orange Bitters 1 dash Complements the smoke and orange peel.
Orange Peel 1 strip Garnish.
Large Ice Cube 1 large
Smoking Wood Chips Pinch Cherry, pecan, oak, or hickory.

Instructions

  1. Combine: In an Old Fashioned glass, add Demerara syrup, Angostura, and Orange bitters. Add whiskey.
  2. Smoke: Using a handheld smoker or smoking gun, introduce smoke into the glass. Swirl to infuse. (Alternatively, smoke a separate cloche/cover and place it over the drink after assembly).
  3. Ice & Stir: Add large ice cube. Stir for 30-45 seconds until well-chilled and slightly diluted.
  4. Garnish: Express orange peel.

2. The Mezcal Old Fashioned (Smoky & Spicy) ⭐⭐⭐⭐

A modern, smoky twist for agave spirit lovers.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Mezcal 2 oz Go for a quality Espadín mezcal with a good balance of smoke.
Agave Nectar ¼ oz Thins out mezcal’s natural sweetness.
Mole Bitters 2 dashes Or chocolate bitters if mole isn’t available. Essential for depth.
Orange Bitters 1 dash Brightens the spice.
Orange Peel 1 strip Garnish.
Large Ice Cube 1 large

Instructions

  1. Combine: In an Old Fashioned glass, add agave nectar, mole bitters, and orange bitters. Add mezcal.
  2. Stir: Stir briefly to combine.
  3. Ice & Stir Again: Add large ice cube. Stir for 30 seconds.
  4. Garnish: Express orange peel.

3. The Maple Old Fashioned (Autumnal & Rich) ⭐⭐⭐

Perfect for cooler weather or a sweeter palate.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Rye Whiskey (or robust Bourbon) 2 oz Rye’s spice pairs wonderfully with maple.
Maple Syrup (Grade A Dark) ¼ - ½ oz Adjust to your sweetness preference. Avoid “pancake syrup.”
Peychaud’s Bitters 2 dashes Adds a nice anise note and a hint of pink hue.
Angostura Bitters 1 dash For balance.
Lemon Peel 1 strip A brighter aroma to complement the maple.
Large Ice Cube 1 large

Instructions

  1. Combine: In an Old Fashioned glass, add maple syrup, Peychaud’s, and Angostura bitters. Add whiskey.
  2. Stir: Stir briefly.
  3. Ice & Stir Again: Add large ice cube. Stir for 40-60 seconds.
  4. Garnish: Express lemon peel.

Tips for a Perfect Old Fashioned

  • Chill Your Glass: A cold glass keeps your drink colder longer.
  • Stir, Don’t Shake: This ensures proper, controlled dilution and a velvety texture.
  • Quality Ice: Large, dense ice melts slower, preserving the integrity of your drink.
  • Taste as You Go: Everyone’s palate is different. Adjust sweetness and dilution to your liking.
  • Experiment with Bitters: There’s a whole world beyond Angostura!
  • Fresh Citrus: Always use fresh orange or lemon peel for garnish. The oils are key.

Enjoy your journey into the world of Old Fashioneds! May your drinks be stirred, not shaken.
