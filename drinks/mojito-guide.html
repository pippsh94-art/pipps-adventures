The Mojito: Cuba's Minty Masterpiece 🌿

Fresh mint, white rum, lime, and soda - the ultimate summer refresher

The Art of the Mojito

The Mojito is summer in a glass - refreshing, aromatic, and utterly satisfying. This Cuban classic requires fresh mint, gentle muddling technique, and quality white rum. When made properly, it's bright, herbaceous, and perfectly balanced between sweet, sour, and effervescent.

The Mojito Foundation

  • The Mint: Fresh spearmint - the star ingredient
  • The Rum: Light/white rum that won't overpower the mint
  • The Citrus: Fresh lime juice and lime wedges
  • The Sweet: Simple syrup or raw sugar
  • The Sparkle: Club soda to lengthen and refresh
  • The Technique: Gentle muddling preserves mint oils

The Classic Mojito

The traditional Cuban recipe that conquered the world.

Ingredients & Ratios

Ingredient Amount Notes
Fresh Mint Leaves 8-10 Spearmint preferred, avoid bruising
Fresh Lime Juice ¾ oz From about ½ lime
Simple Syrup ½ oz Or 1 tsp raw sugar
White Rum 2 oz Cuban-style rum ideal
Club Soda 2-3 oz Top to fill glass
Mint Sprig 1 large Aromatic garnish
Lime Wheel 1 Visual garnish

Instructions

  1. Muddle Gently: In highball glass, lightly muddle mint with lime juice and syrup
  2. Add Rum: Pour white rum over muddled mixture
  3. Fill with Ice: Add crushed or cubed ice to fill glass ¾
  4. Top with Soda: Fill with club soda, stir gently
  5. Garnish: Top with mint sprig and lime wheel
  6. Serve: With straw and stirring spoon

Mojito Technique Secrets

  • Mint Selection: Choose spearmint over peppermint - it's less aggressive
  • Gentle Muddling: Press and twist, don't smash - avoid bitter stems
  • Muddling Order: Mint + lime juice + sugar, then rum
  • Ice Choice: Crushed ice looks authentic, cubes work fine
  • Soda Temperature: Ice-cold club soda maintains carbonation
  • Garnish Prep: Slap mint sprig to release oils before garnishing

Common Mojito Mistakes

  • Over-muddling: Destroys mint and makes drink bitter
  • Wrong mint: Peppermint is too strong
  • Flat soda: Use fresh club soda, add last
  • Too much rum: Should taste refreshing, not boozy
  • Skipping garnish: Mint sprig is essential for aroma
  • Wrong glass: Needs tall glass for proper ice and soda ratio

Mojito Pro Tips

  • Mint Storage: Keep mint stems in water like flowers
  • Pre-batch Base: Make mint-lime-sugar mixture hours ahead
  • Double Strain: Remove mint pieces for cleaner drink
  • Rim Sugar: Light sugar rim enhances presentation
  • Proper Dilution: Some water from ice melt is essential
  • Straw Placement: Through mint garnish for aromatics with each sip

The Mojito is about balance and freshness - the cooling mint, bright lime, smooth rum, and effervescent soda should work in perfect harmony. Take time with the muddling, use quality ingredients, and you'll understand why this Cuban classic has become a worldwide favorite.
