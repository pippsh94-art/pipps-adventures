The Whiskey Sour: The Perfect Introduction 🥃

Sweet, sour, strong - the crowd-pleasing classic that wins over everyone

Mastering the Whiskey Sour

The Whiskey Sour is the gateway drug to whiskey cocktails. It balances spirit with sweet and sour, creating something approachable yet sophisticated. Add an egg white, and it becomes restaurant-quality. Skip it, and it's still delicious.

The Whiskey Sour Blueprint

  • The Whiskey: Bourbon is most popular, rye adds spice
  • The Acid: Fresh lemon juice - never bottled
  • The Sweet: Simple syrup or sugar
  • The Foam: Egg white optional but recommended
  • The Method: Shake hard for froth and proper dilution
  • The Garnish: Cherry and orange wheel traditional

The Classic Whiskey Sour

The foundation recipe that everything builds from.

Ingredients & Ratios

Ingredient Amount Notes
Bourbon 2 oz Buffalo Trace, Maker's Mark, or Wild Turkey 101
Fresh Lemon Juice ¾ oz Freshly squeezed only
Simple Syrup ¾ oz 1:1 sugar to water
Egg White 1 Optional but transforms the drink
Cherry & Orange 1 each Maraschino cherry and orange wheel

Instructions

  1. Dry Shake: Combine all ingredients in shaker WITHOUT ice, shake vigorously 10 seconds (if using egg white)
  2. Wet Shake: Add ice, shake again vigorously 10-15 seconds
  3. Double Strain: Strain through fine mesh into coupe or rocks glass
  4. Garnish: Add cherry and orange wheel

Whiskey Sour Variations

1. New York Sour ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Red wine float creates a stunning visual and adds depth.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Rye Whiskey 2 oz Spicier whiskey pairs with wine
Fresh Lemon Juice ¾ oz Fresh squeezed
Simple Syrup ¾ oz Standard sweetener
Egg White 1 Essential for this version
Red Wine ½ oz float Cabernet or Malbec work well

2. Gold Rush ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Honey syrup instead of simple - richer, rounder sweetness.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Bourbon 2 oz Higher proof works well (100+)
Fresh Lemon Juice ¾ oz Balances honey sweetness
Honey Syrup ¾ oz 3:1 honey to water ratio
Lemon Wheel 1 Simple garnish

3. Paper Plane ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Equal parts modern classic with bourbon and Italian liqueurs.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Bourbon ¾ oz Part of equal parts ratio
Aperol ¾ oz Bitter orange aperitif
Amaro Nonino ¾ oz Honey-sweetened amaro
Fresh Lemon Juice ¾ oz Bright acid component

4. Amaretto Sour ⭐⭐⭐

Almond liqueur base with bourbon float.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Amaretto 1½ oz Disaronno or higher quality
Bourbon ½ oz Adds depth to sweet amaretto
Fresh Lemon Juice 1 oz More acid to balance sweetness
Egg White 1 Creates nice foam
Brandied Cherries 2 Luxury garnish

5. Penicillin ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Scotch sour with ginger and honey - modern classic.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Blended Scotch 1¾ oz Compass Box, Monkey Shoulder
Islay Scotch ¼ oz float Ardbeg or Laphroaig for smoke
Fresh Lemon Juice ¾ oz Essential acid
Honey-Ginger Syrup ¾ oz Muddle fresh ginger in honey syrup
Candied Ginger 1 piece Garnish and spice

6. Brown Derby ⭐⭐⭐

Bourbon, grapefruit, and honey - refreshing variation.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Bourbon 2 oz Standard base
Fresh Grapefruit Juice 1 oz Pink grapefruit preferred
Honey Syrup ½ oz Balances grapefruit bitterness
Grapefruit Twist 1 Express oils over drink

Whiskey Sour Techniques

  • Egg White Safety: Use pasteurized eggs or skip if concerned
  • Dry Shake First: Creates better foam texture
  • Shake Hard: Vigorous shaking creates proper dilution and foam
  • Double Strain: Removes ice chips and ensures smooth texture
  • Fresh Citrus: Juice lemons the day you use them
  • Simple Syrup: Make large batches, keeps in fridge for weeks

Whiskey Sour Pro Tips

  • Balance: Adjust sweet/sour ratio to taste - start with equal parts
  • Whiskey Proof: Higher proof (100+) stands up better to dilution
  • Garnish Prep: Express citrus oils over foam before garnishing
  • Glass Choice: Coupe shows off foam, rocks glass for casual
  • Make It Ahead: Everything but ice can be pre-mixed hours ahead
  • Scaling Up: Easy to batch for parties - just multiply

The Whiskey Sour is cocktail comfort food - familiar, satisfying, and endlessly adaptable. Whether you go classic or creative, the foundation remains the same: good whiskey, fresh citrus, and proper technique.
