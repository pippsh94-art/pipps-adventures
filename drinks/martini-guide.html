The Martini: Gin's Greatest Expression 🍸

The king of cocktails - cold, clear, and absolutely perfect

The Art of the Martini

The Martini is more than a drink - it's an icon, a statement, a meditation on simplicity. With just gin, vermouth, and garnish, every element must be perfect. There's no hiding behind mixers or modifiers. It's gin, elevated.

The Martini Fundamentals

  • The Gin: London Dry is classic, but any quality gin works. The gin is the star.
  • The Vermouth: Dry (white) vermouth adds complexity and rounds the edges.
  • The Ratio: From 2:1 to bone dry - personal preference rules.
  • The Temperature: Ice cold. Non-negotiable.
  • The Garnish: Olive or lemon twist - each creates a different drink.

The Classic Dry Martini

The template that launched a thousand variations.

Ingredients & Ratios

Ingredient Amount Notes
London Dry Gin 2½ oz Tanqueray, Beefeater, or Bombay
Dry Vermouth ½ oz Dolin Dry or Noilly Prat (5:1 ratio)
Lemon Twist or Olives 1 Choose your camp

Instructions

  1. Chill Everything: Glass in freezer, gin and vermouth in fridge
  2. Combine: Add gin and vermouth to mixing glass
  3. Stir: Add ice, stir for 30-40 seconds until very cold
  4. Strain: Strain into chilled coupe or martini glass
  5. Garnish: Express lemon twist oils over drink, or add 1-3 olives

Martini Variations

1. Extra Dry Martini ⭐⭐⭐⭐

For gin purists - just a whisper of vermouth.

Ingredient Amount Notes
London Dry Gin 2¾ oz Premium gin recommended
Dry Vermouth ¼ oz (or less) Some just rinse the glass
Lemon Twist 1 Essential with less vermouth

2. Dirty Martini ⭐⭐⭐

Olive brine adds salinity and umami.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Gin or Vodka 2½ oz Vodka is more common for dirty
Dry Vermouth ½ oz Standard amount
Olive Brine ½ oz Quality olives = quality brine
Green Olives 2-3 Castelvetrano or Cerignola

3. Gibson ⭐⭐⭐⭐

A martini with cocktail onions - surprisingly different flavor.

Ingredient Amount Notes
London Dry Gin 2½ oz Botanical gin works well
Dry Vermouth ½ oz Classic ratio
Cocktail Onions 2-3 Quality makes a difference

4. Vesper Martini ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

James Bond's creation - gin, vodka, and Lillet Blanc.

Ingredient Amount Notes
London Dry Gin 2 oz High-proof gin preferred
Vodka 1 oz Good quality, neutral
Lillet Blanc ½ oz French aperitif wine
Lemon Twist 1 large Express oils generously

5. Perfect Martini ⭐⭐⭐

Half dry, half sweet vermouth for complexity.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Gin 2½ oz Botanical gin shines here
Dry Vermouth ¼ oz Half the usual
Sweet Vermouth ¼ oz Adds richness
Orange Twist 1 Complements sweet vermouth

6. Vodka Martini ⭐⭐⭐

Smoother, less botanical - a different animal entirely.

Ingredient Amount Notes
Premium Vodka 2½ oz Tito's, Grey Goose, or Belvedere
Dry Vermouth ½ oz Don't skip it entirely
Lemon Twist or Olives 1 Personal preference

Martini Philosophy & Technique

  • Stir, Don't Shake: Unless you're Bond, stirring gives proper texture
  • Temperature is King: Everything should be as cold as possible
  • Vermouth Matters: Fresh vermouth (refrigerated) makes a huge difference
  • Glass Choice: Coupe stays colder longer than V-shaped martini glass
  • Garnish Impact: Lemon = bright/citrusy, Olive = briny/savory
  • Personal Preference: Find your ideal gin-to-vermouth ratio
  • Quality Over Quantity: Better to have one perfect martini than three mediocre ones

Common Martini Ratios

  • Classic: 5:1 (gin to vermouth)
  • Wet: 3:1 or 4:1
  • Dry: 7:1 or 8:1
  • Extra Dry: 15:1 or just a vermouth rinse
  • Bone Dry: Gin with no vermouth (not technically a Martini)

The Martini is cocktail perfection - simple ingredients executed flawlessly. Find your preferred ratio, use quality spirits, keep everything ice cold, and you'll understand why this drink has captivated drinkers for over a century.
