## What to Look For When Buying

### Body & Frame

- [ ] **Steel or fiberglass body?** â€” Ask directly. Magnet test (magnet won't stick to fiberglass). Steel is worth more.
- [ ] **Reproduction body?** â€” Many "steel" bodies are actually new Brookville or Dearborn Deuce reproductions (which are excellent quality but should be priced accordingly)
- [ ] **Original Henry Ford steel?** â€” The real deal. VIN on the frame. Worth significantly more.
- [ ] **Frame** â€” Original rails, aftermarket (TCI, Chassis Engineering, Pete & Jakes, SO-CAL), or custom? Aftermarket is fine â€” often better.
- [ ] **Rust** â€” Check floors, firewall, lower quarters, trunk area. '32s are 90+ years old. Some rust is expected.
- [ ] **Body filler** â€” Magnet test or paint thickness gauge. Some is fine, tons is a red flag.
- [ ] **Gaps and alignment** â€” Doors, hood, trunk lid should fit evenly.

### Engine (Flathead V8)

- [ ] **Which flathead?** â€” 221ci (1932â€“38), 239ci (1939â€“48), 255ci (Mercury 49â€“53). Ask for specifics.
- [ ] **Rebuild history** â€” When was it last rebuilt? By whom? Receipts/documentation?
- [ ] **Compression test** â€” Should be 90â€“120 PSI per cylinder, even across all 8
- [ ] **Oil pressure** â€” Should be 30â€“45 PSI warm at idle
- [ ] **Cooling system** â€” Modern aluminum radiator? Electric fan? Overheating history?
- [ ] **Exhaust leaks** â€” Listen carefully. Flatheads develop cracks.
- [ ] **Speed equipment** â€” What heads, cam, intake, carbs? Edelbrock and Offenhauser equipment is desirable.

### Transmission (5-Speed)

- [ ] **Which 5-speed?** â€” Tremec TKO is ideal. T-5 is okay for stock power.
- [ ] **Adapter kit** â€” Wilcap or other quality adapter?
- [ ] **Clutch** â€” Hydraulic throwout bearing is ideal (no clutch linkage headaches)
- [ ] **Shifts smoothly** â€” Drive it through all gears. No grinding, popping out of gear.
- [ ] **Overdrive works** â€” Get it on the highway and confirm 5th gear drops RPMs

### Brakes, Suspension, Steering

- [ ] **Brakes** â€” Disc fronts are a must for safety. Disc rears are nice. Drum-only is sketchy for modern driving.
- [ ] **Steering** â€” Vega box, rack-and-pinion, or cross-steer? Should be tight, no wandering.
- [ ] **Front suspension** â€” Dropped axle (traditional) or independent? Both are fine. IFS is smoother.
- [ ] **Rear end** â€” Ford 9-inch, 8-inch, or early Ford banjo? 9-inch is best.

### Electrical

- [ ] **12-volt conversion?** â€” Should be. 6-volt is a hassle.
- [ ] **Wiring quality** â€” Spaghetti wiring = fire hazard. Look for quality harness (Painless, American Autowire).
- [ ] **Turn signals, brake lights, headlights** â€” All working? You need them for road driving.

### Documentation & Title

- [ ] **Clean title** â€” Some states title these as "assembled vehicles" or "replica." Know your state's rules (Idaho is pretty friendly for hot rods).
- [ ] **Build documentation** â€” Photos, receipts, builder info. Adds value and trust.
