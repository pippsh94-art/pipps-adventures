July 4th Weekend 2026 — Treasure Valley & Gem County Events 🎆

July 3–5, 2026 | Centered on Star, Idaho   July 4th falls on a Saturday

🇺🇸 America 250: 2026 marks the 250th anniversary of American independence. Many Treasure Valley celebrations are larger than usual — extra fireworks, drone shows, and "America 250"-themed events.

⭐ Star, Idaho

Star Hometown Celebration & Parade

  • Date: Saturday, July 4
  • Parade: Typically 9:30–11:30 AM down State Street
  • Where: Downtown Star (State Street corridor & Star Riverside Park)
  • Full-day program includes:
    • Firecracker Fun Run (early morning)
    • Hometown parade down State Street (floats, fire trucks, horses, local businesses)
    • Splash zone for kids
    • Pie contest
    • Food vendors and local booths at Riverside Park
    • Live music and family activities
    • Community fireworks show after dark (~10:00 PM)
  • Tips: Get there early for parade spots. Bring chairs & blankets. Star's celebration has grown significantly with the town — expect a great turnout.

🦅 Eagle

⚠️ Eagle Fun Days 2026 — Heads Up: Different Weekend

Eagle Fun Days 2026 is scheduled for June 27–28, 2026, NOT July 4th weekend. 2026 is the 55th anniversary of Eagle Fun Days and is "America 250" themed (red, white, and blue dress code encouraged). The classic parade, vendor markets, live music, fun run, and ball drop happen the weekend BEFORE July 4 in 2026. If you want Eagle Fun Days, plan it as a separate June trip.

Eagle on July 4th Weekend

  • Eagle Island State Park is open all weekend — swimming, paddleboarding, picnicking
  • Downtown Eagle businesses and restaurants are open with patio seating
  • No major Eagle-hosted parade or fireworks on July 4 itself in 2026 (those happened the previous weekend)
  • Distance from Star: ~10 minutes

🏙️ Boise — America 250 Special Edition

4th of July Fireworks Celebration — Ann Morrison Park

  • Date: Saturday, July 4
  • Festivities begin: 6:00 PM
  • Boise Philharmonic patriotic performance: Free, evening (precedes the fireworks)
  • Drone light show: ~10:20 PM (NEW for America 250)
  • Fireworks: ~10:15 PM at dusk
  • Where: Ann Morrison Park, Boise
  • What to expect:
    • Boise's premier fireworks show — massive display over the park
    • Free Boise Philharmonic performance (special for America 250)
    • Synchronized drone show following the fireworks
    • Food trucks and vendors surround the park
    • Live entertainment leading up to the show
    • Thousands attend — huge community gathering
  • ⚠️ CRITICAL — Park is closed to vehicles all day: Ann Morrison Park is vehicle-closed Saturday, July 4. You must walk, bike, or rideshare in. Plan accordingly.
  • Distance from Star: ~20 minutes drive (then walk/bike from designated drop-off areas)
  • Tips: Park outside the closed zone and walk in. Bring blankets. Plan your exit strategy in advance — gridlock leaving the area is normal.

Boise River Float

  • Date: All weekend (weather permitting)
  • Time: 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM (last shuttle ~6 PM)
  • Where: Barber Park to Ann Morrison Park (6 miles)
  • What to expect:
    • Rent tubes, rafts, or kayaks at Barber Park
    • 2–3 hour lazy float down the Boise River
    • Peak holiday vibes — everyone is out
  • Cost: ~$20–30 for tube rental + shuttle
  • Tips: Go early (before noon) to avoid the huge afternoon crowds. Waterproof your phone. Bring sunscreen. Note: Ann Morrison takeout will be busy with fireworks setup later.

Boise 4th of July Parade

  • Date: Saturday, July 4 (morning)
  • Where: Downtown Boise
  • America 250 special: Larger than usual for the 250th anniversary
  • Check: boise4th.com for the current year's route and start time

🌟 Meridian

Meridian July 4th Activities

  • Date: Saturday, July 4
  • Where: Various parks around Meridian
  • What to expect:
    • Community celebrations in neighborhoods
    • The Village at Meridian often hosts patriotic entertainment and dining specials
    • Roaring Springs Water Park — open for the holiday (expect huge crowds)

🏔️ Emmett (Gem County)

Emmett Independence Day Celebration

  • Date: Saturday, July 4
  • Time: Parade ~11:00 AM; festivities all day
  • Where: City Park & Main Street, Emmett
  • What to expect:
    • Small-town parade down Main Street (very Americana)
    • Community BBQ and potluck atmosphere
    • Live music in the park
    • Fireworks at dusk (~10:00 PM) — typically launched from near the Gem County Fairgrounds
    • Horseshoe tournaments, sack races, kids' games
  • Distance from Star: ~30 minutes (scenic drive through Freezeout Hill or Beacon Light Rd)
  • Tips: This is classic small-town July 4th. Less crowded than Boise, more intimate. Great for a laid-back vibe.

Gem County Fairgrounds Area

  • Where: Emmett
  • What to expect:
    • Sometimes hosts additional rodeo events around July 4th weekend
    • Check for Gem County Rodeo — often scheduled near Independence Day
    • Arena events, barrel racing, bull riding

🎆 Nampa

Nampa Festival of Freedom

  • Date: Saturday, July 4
  • Time: All day, fireworks ~10:00 PM
  • Where: Lakeview Park & downtown Nampa
  • What to expect:
    • One of the bigger July 4th festivals in the valley
    • Car show (usually morning/midday)
    • Live music on multiple stages
    • Food vendors, craft booths, beer garden
    • Kids' zone with inflatables and activities
    • Major fireworks display over the lake at ~10:00 PM
  • Distance from Star: ~25 minutes
  • Tips: Lakeview Park has great fireworks viewing from the grass. Nampa goes all-out and has FAR less traffic chaos than Ann Morrison.

Caldwell July 4th Celebration

Indian Creek Winery & Sunny Slope Area

🏖️ Lake Lowell / Deer Flat

Lake Lowell July 4th

  • Date: All weekend
  • Where: Lake Lowell, south of Nampa
  • What to do:
    • Boating, kayaking, paddleboarding
    • Picnic areas along the shore
    • Fishing (bass, crappie, bluegill)
    • Birdwatching at Deer Flat National Wildlife Refuge
  • Tips: Great way to spend the daytime before evening fireworks. Bring shade — it's hot in July.

📌 Pro Tips

  1. Star parade is your hometown must-do — walk there if you can, parking gets tight on State Street
  2. ⚠️ Eagle Fun Days is June 27–28 in 2026, NOT July 4 weekend — plan it as a separate trip if you want it
  3. Boise's Ann Morrison is vehicle-closed all day July 4 — walk, bike, or rideshare in. The America 250 drone show + Philharmonic is the biggest show in the valley but logistically complicated.
  4. Nampa's Lakeview Park is the easier alternative for big-show fireworks — less traffic chaos than Ann Morrison
  5. Emmett is the hidden gem (pun intended) — true small-town July 4th, way less crowded
  6. Hydrate aggressively — July in the valley is no joke, 100°F is common
  7. Fireworks viewing hack: If you don't want crowds, watch Boise's or Nampa's from a distance — several foothills and elevated spots in Star/Eagle have sightlines to multiple shows
  8. Book the river float early — July 4th weekend sells out for tube rentals
  9. Early mornings for outdoor activities — by 11 AM the heat is brutal in July
